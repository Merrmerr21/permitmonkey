## The Thesis

Massachusetts legalized ADUs by-right statewide on February 2, 2025 via **Chapter 150 of the Acts of 2024** (the Affordable Homes Act). This created a new housing market that did not exist 14 months ago. The Healey-Driscoll administration projects **8,000–10,000 ADUs** will be built across MA over the next five years.

No entrenched permit-assist tool has staked MA territory. Contractors, architects, and homeowners are all learning the new rules simultaneously. That is a distribution opportunity.

## Why MA Beats CA (Strategic Comparison)

| Dimension | California | Massachusetts |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| ADU law status | Mature (~10 years statewide) | 14 months old |
| Market size | ~400K+ permits since 2018 | Projected 8–10K over 5 years |
| Competitor density | Many entrenched tools, lawyers, consultants | Effectively zero dedicated ADU permit tools |
| Regulatory stability | Constant updates (SB 9, AB 1033, etc.) | Stable post-Ch 150 |
| Target-customer sophistication | Already AI-skeptical, many paid alternatives | Early-adopter window, high need |
| Merritt's network | Minimal | Strong (JLL Boston, MA RE license studies, Boston-based) |

Merritt's advantages compound in MA. They don't in CA.

### Tertiary (Roadmap)

## Market Size Math (MA)

Back-of-envelope:
- ~9,000 ADUs over 5 years = ~1,800/year statewide
- Boston metro concentration: ~60% of volume = ~1,080 Boston metro ADUs/year
- Corrections rate: assume 60% of submissions receive corrections = ~648 corrections jobs/year in Boston metro alone
- Average corrections cycle: 2–3 rounds × ~3 hours each = 6–9 hours of contractor time
- Price anchor: $400–800 per corrections-response package

At a 20% market share of Boston metro corrections jobs: ~130 jobs/year × $500 = **$65K ARR** just from Boston metro corrections. Multiply by statewide coverage + checklist product + city-side product + free-tool referrals and the path to 7-figures is credible.

## Positioning Statement

"Get your Massachusetts ADU permit through plan check in half the cycles. PermitMonkey reads your corrections letter, cross-references state and local code, and drafts your response package — so you can focus on building, not citing statutes."

## Differentiation

- **Only MA-specific permit tool** — generic AI tools don't know MGL Ch 40A §3 as amended or 760 CMR 71.00
- **Citation integrity** — zero-tolerance hallucination policy; every citation has a verifiable URL
- **State preemption awareness** — spots when a city correction is unenforceable; contractor gets a polite but firm response that stands up
- **City-specific gotchas** — Boston, Cambridge, Somerville first; Newton, Brookline, Worcester, Quincy, Lowell next
- **Hackathon pedigree** — won Anthropic's Built with Opus 4.6 hackathon (credential + model access)

## What We Are Not

- Not a general permit tool (ADU-specific, for now)
- Not a legal-advice tool (disclaimed; Opus has a zero-tolerance citation policy but we are not lawyers)
- Not a design tool (architects handle design; we handle the permit paperwork)
- Not for commercial / multifamily / industrial (residential SFR-zone ADU focus)
- Not outside Massachusetts (for now)

## Anti-Slop Discipline (Content)

Per `PLAYBOOK.md` §31 and the Anti-Slop addendum in the original playbook source:

- Human-written content outperforms AI-generated content on engagement
- If using AI as a starting point, fully rewrite before posting
- Avoid em dashes, "delve", "in today's fast-paced world," teleprompter delivery in video
- Use AI for structure and research; the voice must be Merritt's
