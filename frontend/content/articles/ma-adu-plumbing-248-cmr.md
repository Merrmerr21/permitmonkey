# Massachusetts ADU Plumbing Under 248 CMR

The Massachusetts plumbing code is its own regulation — 248 CMR — administered by the Board of State Examiners of Plumbers and Gas Fitters, separate from 780 CMR (the State Building Code). For an ADU permit, this means plumbing is reviewed and inspected on a separate track from the building permit, often by a different inspector. This guide walks through what 248 CMR requires for ADU work, the four-permit workflow most projects follow, BWSC coordination requirements (Boston-specific), and the corrections plan checkers most often issue.

## The regulation and its authority

[source: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/248-CMR | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 248 CMR — MA Uniform State Plumbing and Gas Code]

[source: https://www.mass.gov/orgs/board-of-state-examiners-of-plumbers-and-gas-fitters | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: Board of State Examiners — authority over 248 CMR]

248 CMR Chapter 10 governs plumbing. Chapters 4-7 govern gas fitting (gas lines and gas appliance connections). Both apply to ADU work; gas fitting only when fuel-burning appliances are present.

The Board administers the code statewide. Local plumbing inspectors enforce. A Massachusetts master plumber must hold a license and pull the plumbing permit in the homeowner's or contractor's name.

## The four-permit workflow

A typical Massachusetts ADU project requires four separate permits issued at different times by different inspectors:

1. **Building permit** — issued by the local building department under 780 CMR. Covers structural, framing, fire separation, accessibility, energy code compliance.
2. **Plumbing permit** — issued by the local plumbing inspector under 248 CMR. Covers all water supply, drain-waste-vent, and fixture work.
3. **Gas permit** (if applicable) — issued by the local gas inspector under 248 CMR. Covers gas lines and gas appliance connections. Most Specialized Opt-In Code ADUs are all-electric and skip this permit.
4. **Electrical permit** — issued by the local electrical inspector under 527 CMR 12. Covers all wiring, panel work, and service connections.

Each permit has its own application, fee, and inspection schedule. Coordination between the four trades is the contractor's responsibility. The building inspector can sign off on the building permit while the plumbing or gas permits are still in progress; final certificate of occupancy requires all four signed off.

## Drain sizing — DFU calculations

The fundamental plumbing math for an ADU is fixture-unit calculation. Each fixture has a Drainage Fixture Unit (DFU) value, and the building drain has to be sized to handle the cumulative DFU load.

Typical fixture DFU values per 248 CMR Chapter 10:

- **Water closet (toilet)**: 4 DFU
- **Lavatory (bathroom sink)**: 1 DFU
- **Shower or tub**: 2 DFU
- **Kitchen sink with disposal**: 2-3 DFU
- **Dishwasher**: 1.5 DFU
- **Clothes washer**: 3 DFU

A typical ADU with a full bath, kitchen, and laundry runs about 12-15 DFU.

Drain pipe sizing per 248 CMR 10.10 Table 10-3:

- **2 inch**: up to 6 DFU (typical for branches serving lavs, showers)
- **3 inch**: up to 27 DFU (typical for the building drain serving the toilet and kitchen)
- **4 inch**: up to 216 DFU (typical for the lateral connecting to the city sewer)

Most ADU connections use a 4 inch lateral to the existing house lateral or directly to the city main. The 4 inch size has substantial headroom over what a single ADU needs.

## Water service sizing

Water service from the city main to the parcel must be sized for the combined demand of the primary dwelling and the ADU. Typical existing single-family residential service is 3/4 inch or 1 inch.

For an ADU addition, the existing service may need an upgrade depending on the combined fixture demand. Common upgrade scenarios:

- **3/4 inch existing, ADU added**: Often acceptable for a small ADU if the primary is modest. Verify against 248 CMR Chapter 5 Water Supply Distribution sizing tables.
- **1 inch upgrade required**: Common when the primary already has substantial fixture demand and the ADU adds significant draw.
- **Subpanel only, no upgrade**: Some ADUs feed off an internal subpanel with no service upgrade — works only if the existing service has documented headroom.

The water-service sizing decision is a plumbing-engineer-or-master-plumber call based on the specific fixture demand. Plan reviewers typically accept the master plumber's stamped sizing without second-guessing.

## BWSC coordination — Boston-specific

For Boston ADU projects, the **Boston Water and Sewer Commission (BWSC)** is the city water and sewer authority. New lateral connections require BWSC approval and inspection independently of the city plumbing permit.

The BWSC workflow:

1. **Service Application** filed with BWSC for a new water service or new sewer lateral connection.
2. **BWSC engineering review** of the proposed connection.
3. **Cut sheet issued** authorizing the contractor to make the cut into the public way.
4. **Coordination with other utilities** — gas, electric, telecom — for the trench work.
5. **BWSC field inspection** of the connection before backfill.
6. **As-built submission** documenting the actual configuration.

BWSC coordination adds 4-8 weeks to an ADU permit timeline if a new lateral is required. Plan for it in the schedule.

For non-Boston cities, the local DPW or water/sewer authority has analogous processes. The principles are similar; the names and forms differ.

## Common ADU plumbing corrections

Plan checkers under 248 CMR commonly issue:

- **"Show DFU calculation for the building drain."** Add a fixture-unit table to the plumbing plan with cumulative DFUs and the drain-sizing rationale.
- **"Specify lateral connection point and method."** Note where the ADU lateral connects to the existing house lateral or to the city main, and specify the connection method (wye fitting, two-way cleanout, slope, materials).
- **"Identify cleanout locations."** 248 CMR requires cleanouts at specific points along the drain — at the building drain start, at every 100 feet, at changes of direction over 45 degrees.
- **"Show vent system."** Every fixture must connect to a vent. Show the vent stack and any branch vents serving multiple fixtures.
- **"Specify materials."** PVC, ABS, copper, PEX, cast iron — each has approved uses under 248 CMR. State the material on the plan.

## All-electric ADUs and the gas permit

For Specialized Opt-In Code municipalities (Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline, etc.), the practical compliance path for new construction is all-electric — no gas, no oil, no propane. This eliminates the gas permit entirely.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/specialized-stretch-code | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC — Specialized Opt-In Energy Code disincentivizes fossil fuels]

For projects where gas is desired (e.g., a gas range despite all-electric heating), the gas permit applies under 248 CMR Chapters 4-7 administered by a licensed gas fitter. The combined permit count goes from four to four (still: building, plumbing, gas, electrical).

## The cross-reference to 780 CMR

780 CMR (the State Building Code) cross-references 248 CMR for all plumbing work. The building plan reviewer typically does not second-guess plumbing-code compliance; that's the plumbing inspector's job.

The building plan does need to show:

- The location of plumbing fixtures on the floor plan
- The wall locations where plumbing chases run (for framing review)
- Any structural impact of plumbing work (cantilevers for kitchen sinks, headers for bathroom relocations)

The detailed plumbing schematic, DFU calculation, and pipe-sizing logic live on the plumbing plan submitted with the plumbing permit, not the building permit.

## What good ADU plumbing plans include

- Plumbing fixture schedule with DFU values per fixture
- Cumulative DFU calculation for each branch and the building drain
- Drain pipe sizing per branch (2", 3", 4")
- Vent system layout
- Cleanout locations marked on plan
- Water service connection point and size
- Lateral connection detail (BWSC coordination if Boston, equivalent for other cities)
- Materials specification per pipe section
- Master plumber's stamp and signature
