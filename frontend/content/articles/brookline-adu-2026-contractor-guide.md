# Brookline ADU 2026 Contractor Guide

Brookline is a town, not a city, with town meeting governance — meaning bylaw amendments require approval from a several-hundred-member elected legislative body that meets twice yearly. This affects how quickly Brookline can update its ADU bylaw to align with Chapter 150 (slowly) and the kinds of disputes that arise (more procedural, more entrenched).

This guide walks through Brookline's S-district dimensional standards, the FAR caps that bind tighter than the state floor, the Preservation Commission overlays, the Specialized Opt-In Code, and the response template for preempted corrections.

## The state floor that applies to every Brookline parcel

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40A/Section3 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 40A § 3 — by-right ADU paragraph after Ch 150 § 8]

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150 §§ 7-8 — Affordable Homes Act ADU provisions]

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/accessory-dwelling-units | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 760 CMR 71.00 — EOHLC implementing regulation]

Lesser of 900 sqft or 50% of primary. No owner-occupancy. One parking space max, zero within 0.5 miles of qualifying transit. In Brookline, qualifying transit covers most of the town — every Green Line C and D branch stop (Coolidge Corner through Reservoir on the C, Brookline Village through Reservoir on the D), plus key bus station designations on Beacon, Harvard, and Washington Streets.

## Brookline dimensional standards — the S districts

Brookline's residential districts are S-7, S-10, S-15, and several smaller categories. The S-N number indicates the minimum lot size in thousands of square feet:

- **S-7** — minimum lot 7,000 sqft, narrow setbacks, FAR around 0.5
- **S-10** — minimum lot 10,000 sqft, wider setbacks, FAR around 0.4
- **S-15** — minimum lot 15,000 sqft, larger setbacks, FAR tighter still

Brookline's FAR caps bind hardest. With an existing primary at FAR 0.45 in an S-10 zone (cap 0.4 maximum) — wait, the parcel is already non-conforming. ADU additions to non-conforming primaries face additional scrutiny.

[source: https://www.brooklinema.gov/188/Planning-Community-Development | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: Town of Brookline Planning and Community Development — zoning bylaw]

The state floor preempts the by-right protected use. It does not preempt the FAR cap. Plan ahead: calculate combined FAR against the district maximum on the cover sheet.

## The non-conforming primary problem

Brookline's housing stock includes many homes built before current zoning was enacted. These are "pre-existing non-conforming" structures — they exceed current FAR or coverage limits but are grandfathered.

Adding an ADU to a non-conforming primary is doable but requires care:

- The combined non-conforming primary + ADU cannot make the situation "more non-conforming" in the FAR or coverage sense.
- Special-permit relief may be available, but special permits for ADUs themselves are preempted by state law for the by-right protected use. The relief, if needed, is for the dimensional non-conformity, not for the ADU use.
- A clean approach: convert existing space (basement, garage, attic) to the ADU rather than building new floor area. Conversion without expansion typically does not increase non-conformity.

## Preservation Commission overlays

Brookline has multiple local historic districts under MGL Ch 40C, plus the broader Preservation Commission jurisdiction:

- Cottage Farm
- Pill Hill
- Graffam-McKay
- Multiple individual landmarks

Like Newton, Cambridge, Somerville, this authority is **not** preempted by Chapter 150.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40C | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 40C — Historic Districts Act, separate authority not preempted]

Brookline's Preservation Commission has been active on ADU additions. Their review focuses on visible exterior alterations — materials, fenestration, roof pitch, massing relative to neighboring structures. Plan for a 60-120 day review if your parcel sits in a covered district.

## Specialized Opt-In Energy Code adopted

Brookline is on the Specialized Code per 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC. Same path as Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/specialized-stretch-code | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC — Specialized Opt-In Energy Code adopted by Brookline]

All-electric or near-zero fossil fuel. Heat pump space conditioning. High-performance envelope. HERS rating 45-55. Engage a HERS rater early.

## Town meeting governance — what it means for ADU permits

Brookline's bylaw can only be amended at town meeting, which convenes in May and November. This has two practical consequences for ADU work:

1. **Bylaw alignment with Chapter 150 is slow.** Brookline's published bylaw text may not yet reflect state preemption. The state floor applies regardless — the bylaw does not need to be amended for preemption to be operative — but plan checkers may cite outdated bylaw sections.

2. **The Town Counsel and Building Commissioner have interpretive latitude.** When the bylaw is unclear or out of date, the Building Commissioner's interpretation of how state law overlays the bylaw matters. Brookline's Building Commissioner has been responsive to Chapter 150 preemption arguments; cite the state floor cleanly and most reviewers will resolve.

## When Brookline requires conditions preempted by state law

Standard response template:

> "The proposed ADU is a Protected Use under MGL Ch 40A § 3 (as amended by St. 2024, c. 150, § 8) and 760 CMR 71.00. The [parking / owner-occupancy / special-permit] requirement at [Brookline bylaw section] is preempted by state law to the extent it conflicts with the protected-use framework. We respectfully request the comment be withdrawn or revised consistent with state law."

If a Brookline reviewer cites a bylaw section that has not been updated post-Ch 150, the citation may be technically valid in the bylaw but functionally preempted. Ask for the city's interpretive position. The Building Commissioner has the authority to withdraw the comment.

## What good Brookline ADU plan packages include

- Cover sheet protected-use declaration referencing MGL Ch 40A § 3.
- Code summary table: 780 CMR 10th Edition, 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC.
- FAR calculation showing primary + ADU + combined vs the S-district cap. This is non-negotiable in Brookline; missing FAR calculations are an automatic correction.
- Setback dimensions to all property lines.
- Non-conforming-status declaration if the primary is grandfathered. Note the existing FAR vs district cap and confirm the proposed ADU does not increase non-conformity.
- Preservation Commission status declared. If outside any district, confirm by parcel ID.
- Energy compliance path — Specialized Opt-In Code, HERS rater identified.

## Common Brookline-specific gotchas

- **Pre-existing non-conformity** is more common in Brookline than in newer suburbs. Confirm the primary's status before designing the ADU.
- **Town meeting bylaw lag** — Brookline's published bylaw may cite pre-Ch 150 ADU language. The state floor applies regardless; do not be deterred by outdated bylaw text.
- **Preservation Commission strict standards** — even compared to Newton or Cambridge, Brookline's Commission is exacting on visible exterior detail. If your parcel is in a covered district, plan for materials and fenestration choices that match the district's character study.
- **Limited tandem parking acceptability** — Brookline permits tandem (one car behind another) for one of the required spaces in many districts, but the configuration must satisfy minimum dimensional standards. Check the bylaw section for the parcel's district.
