# Can I Build an ADU in Boston? A 2026 Contractor Guide

Boston is in the middle of a multi-year zoning rewrite for ADUs. As of May 2026, one neighborhood (Mattapan) has adopted full ADU regulations under the city's Neighborhood Housing Zoning initiative; three others (Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park) have draft amendments in progress. This guide explains what's currently buildable, what's pending, and the practical path through ISD plan check.

## The framework: Boston is special-act zoning

Most Massachusetts cities operate under MGL Chapter 40A, the standard state zoning enabling act. Boston is the exception. The city operates under a special-act framework — the Boston Zoning Act of 1956 — which means standard Ch 40A naming conventions don't apply.

[source: https://www.boston.gov/departments/inspectional-services | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: Boston operates under a special-act zoning framework, not standard MGL Ch 40A]

Practically, this means:

- The Boston Planning and Development Agency (BPDA) and the Zoning Commission govern zoning, not the City Council via standard amendments.
- Code is hosted on Municode at `library.municode.com/ma/boston/codes/redevelopment_authority`. The platform is JavaScript-rendered, which makes static lookups unreliable; verify Article numbers against the Municode browser interface, not third-party summaries.
- Article numbering doesn't follow Ch 40A patterns. Don't project an Article number from another city's code — it likely doesn't exist in Boston with the same meaning.

## Statewide floor: Chapter 150 of 2024

Boston is still bound by the statewide ADU floor created by **Chapter 150 of the Acts of 2024**, which amended MGL Chapter 40A §§ 1A and 3 to make ADUs by-right in single-family zones across Massachusetts.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150 §§ 7-8 amend MGL Ch 40A §§ 1A and 3]

This is the state law every municipality must work from. The provisions:

- ADUs are by-right in any single-family zone — no special permit, no public hearing, no owner-occupancy requirement.
- Maximum size is the lesser of 900 square feet or 50% of the primary dwelling.
- Up to one parking space may be required; zero parking can be required if the project is within one-half mile of MBTA service.

The implementing regulations are at **760 CMR 71.00**, effective February 2, 2025.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/760-CMR-7100-affordable-homes-act-adu-regulations | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: 760 CMR 71.00 effective 2025-02-02]

## Boston's local layer

Where state law preempts USE (can you build an ADU?), Boston still controls FORM (how big, how setback, how tall). The current state of Boston's local layer is a patchwork:

### Mattapan — adopted February 2024

Mattapan's "PLAN: Mattapan" amendment is the first Boston neighborhood with full ADU regulations adopted under the Neighborhood Housing Zoning initiative. If your project is in Mattapan, the local dimensional standards (setbacks, height limits, lot coverage) come from the Mattapan amendment rather than the underlying neighborhood code.

[source: https://www.bostonplans.org/planning/planning-initiatives/plan-mattapan | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: PLAN: Mattapan amendment adopted February 2024 includes ADU dimensional standards]

### Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park — in draft

As of May 2026, three additional neighborhoods have draft amendments in public review. If your project is in one of these, expect the regulatory environment to change between scoping and submittal — design conservatively to the state floor and verify the local rules at submittal time.

### Other neighborhoods

For neighborhoods without an adopted or draft amendment (Back Bay, South End, Charlestown, etc.), Boston's existing residential zoning provisions apply alongside the state law floor. Most existing neighborhood codes were written before ADUs were by-right under state law, so the dimensional standards in those codes apply but the special-permit requirements they may impose are preempted.

## Dimensional standards (typical)

For neighborhoods without an adopted ADU amendment, expect the following typical Boston dimensional standards (verify against the specific neighborhood code for your address):

- Front setback: 10-15 feet, depending on neighborhood
- Side setback: 5-10 feet
- Rear setback: 10-20 feet (more if abutting a residential lot)
- Maximum building height: 35 feet for residential; 45 feet in some districts
- Lot coverage: 40-60% maximum

These are the dimensions that govern what you can fit on the parcel. The state ADU floor (≤900 sqft, ≤50% of primary) governs what counts as an ADU; the local dimensional standards govern whether your design fits.

## Parking — the MBTA preemption

Boston has high transit coverage. If your parcel is within 0.5 miles (measured on foot via accessible streets) of an MBTA stop, **zero parking can be required for the ADU.**

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150 §7 — parking preemption near MBTA service]

This is one of the most-frequently-disputed corrections in Boston ADU permit reviews. If a plan checker asks for parking near an MBTA stop, the response is to cite the state law preemption with the stop name and walk distance.

## Energy code: Specialized Opt-In

Boston has adopted the Specialized Opt-In Energy Code (225 CMR 22 Appendix RC), the most aggressive of the three Massachusetts energy code tiers.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/stretch-energy-code-development-2023 | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: Boston has adopted the Specialized Opt-In Energy Code]

For a comprehensive walkthrough of the three compliance pathways under Specialized, see our [Massachusetts Stretch Energy Code guide](/articles/ma-stretch-energy-code-explained). The short version: default to all-electric design unless you have a specific reason to deviate.

## Landmarks Commission overlay

Boston has 11 Landmark Districts (Beacon Hill, Back Bay, Bay State Road, St. Botolph, South End, Bay Village, Mission Hill Triangle, Aberdeen, Fort Point Channel, Highland Park, plus the Charlestown Monument Square study).

If your parcel falls in a Landmark District, **the Boston Landmarks Commission's design review applies independently of the state ADU floor.** Chapter 150 of 2024 preempts the city's USE regulation; it does not preempt design review by an independent landmarks commission.

[source: https://www.boston.gov/departments/landmarks-commission | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: Boston Landmarks Commission design review applies in 11 Landmark Districts]

For an ADU in a Landmark District: budget 6-12 weeks of additional review time and design to the district's existing exterior character. A modern infill ADU in Beacon Hill is unlikely to clear Landmarks; a sympathetic addition that reads as period-appropriate is more achievable.

## Building code: 780 CMR 10th Edition

The Massachusetts State Building Code is **780 CMR, 10th Edition**, effective October 2023. Boston applies 780 CMR without significant amendments — Boston ISD enforces the state code as written, with their own plan-review interpretations.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/state-building-code | retrieved: 2026-05-05 | citation: 780 CMR 10th Edition effective October 2023]

If you see a Boston ADU correction citing 780 CMR 9th Edition for a permit dated after October 2023, that's an edition mismatch — flag it and respond with the 10th Edition citation.

## ISD plan-review process

The Boston Inspectional Services Department runs the plan check. Typical timeline for a clean ADU submittal:

- **Initial review**: 2-4 weeks for a complete submittal in a non-Landmark neighborhood.
- **Corrections cycle**: 1-2 cycles is normal; 3+ cycles suggests a scoping issue or a Landmarks complication.
- **Corrections response time**: 1-2 weeks per cycle for the contractor to respond.
- **Permit issuance**: 1 week after the final approval.

Total elapsed time: 8-16 weeks for a typical project. Landmark Districts add 6-12 weeks. Hyde Park / Roslindale / West Roxbury (under draft amendments) may add 2-6 weeks of regulatory uncertainty until the amendments adopt.

## What a Boston ADU corrections letter looks like

The most-frequent items in Boston ISD correction letters on ADU projects in 2024-2026:

1. Parking required within MBTA proximity — cite state preemption.
2. Setback dimension missing or wrong — verify against neighborhood code.
3. Energy code envelope below Specialized — upgrade to Specialized targets.
4. Edition mismatch on 780 CMR — confirm permit date, cite 10th Edition.
5. Landmarks signoff missing (in a Landmark District) — submit to Landmarks before resubmitting to ISD.
6. ADU exceeds 900 sqft — reduce to state floor.
7. ADU exceeds 50% of primary — verify primary square footage; reduce ADU.
8. Owner-occupancy condition — cite state preemption.
9. Special permit required — cite state preemption (by-right under Ch 150).

## What this means for your project

For Boston ADU work in 2026, scope to the state floor first:

- Size: lesser of 900 sqft or 50% of primary. Don't go bigger.
- Parking: zero if within 0.5 mi MBTA, otherwise one space.
- Energy: design all-electric to Specialized.
- Building code: 780 CMR 10th Edition, no Boston amendments.
