# ADU Fire Separation in Massachusetts

Fire-separation corrections are among the most common building-code findings on Massachusetts ADU permits — and most are straightforward to resolve once the controlling provision is identified. This guide walks through the framework that 780 CMR 10th Edition adopts, the distinction between attached and detached ADU separation requirements, and the response patterns for the corrections you are most likely to see.

## The framework: 780 CMR 10th Edition adopting IRC 2021 with MA amendments

The Massachusetts State Building Code, 10th Edition, took effect in October 2023. For one- and two-family dwellings (which includes most ADU work), 780 CMR Chapter 51 adopts the International Residential Code 2021 with Massachusetts amendments. The fire-separation provisions live in IRC R302.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/state-building-code | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 780 CMR — MA State Building Code, 10th Edition, IRC 2021 base with MA amendments]

[source: https://codes.iccsafe.org/ | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: IRC 2021 R302 — fire-resistant construction]

The two operative rules for ADU work:

- **R302.3** governs separation between attached dwelling units. Applies when the ADU shares a wall or floor/ceiling assembly with the primary dwelling.
- **R302.1** governs separation between buildings on the same lot. Applies to detached ADUs based on distance from lot lines and from the primary structure.

Different problem, different rule. Plan checkers often confuse them, and the response sometimes requires re-citing the correct one.

## Attached ADUs — the 1-hour rated assembly

An attached ADU shares one or more building elements with the primary dwelling: a common wall, a floor/ceiling assembly, or both. R302.3 requires a fire-resistance-rated separation between the units.

The MA standard for an attached ADU on an IRC-track project: **1-hour fire-rated assembly** between the dwelling units, with rated through-penetrations and rated assemblies extending continuously through the structure.

For a stacked ADU (ADU above the primary, or vice versa): the floor/ceiling assembly between them must be 1-hour fire-rated. Penetrations (HVAC, plumbing, electrical) require listed firestop systems.

For a side-by-side attached ADU: the common wall must be 1-hour fire-rated. The wall typically extends from the foundation to the underside of the roof deck, with the roof deck assembly handled separately.

Common corrections on attached ADU plans:

- **"Show fire-rated wall assembly with UL listing reference."** Specify the UL design number (typical: UL U305, U309, U419 depending on construction). Show the assembly section with materials and thickness.
- **"Detail rated penetrations."** Show all HVAC, plumbing, and electrical penetrations of the rated assembly with listed firestop systems.
- **"Show rated assembly continuity to roof deck."** The wall must extend continuously; if it terminates at the ceiling, the ceiling must be rated and the attic space must have a rated separation.

## Detached ADUs — distance from lot lines

For a detached ADU as a separate structure on the same lot as the primary, R302.1 controls based on distance to lot lines.

The IRC 2021 framework, as applied in MA:

- **Within 3 feet of a lot line:** exterior walls require 1-hour fire resistance from the inside, openings are not permitted, and projections (eaves, decks) cannot extend past the lot line. Specific requirements for the structural frame, exterior walls, and floors.
- **3 to 5 feet from a lot line:** openings are limited (typically not more than 25% of the wall area), and the wall must have fire-resistance ratings depending on the type of construction.
- **5 feet or more from a lot line:** standard construction acceptable, no additional fire-rating required.

Boundaries are measured to the lot line, not to the neighboring building. The neighboring building's distance does not factor in.

[source: https://codes.iccsafe.org/ | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: IRC 2021 R302.1 Table R302.1(1) — fire-resistance ratings by distance to lot line]

Common corrections on detached ADU plans:

- **"Show fire-separation-distance dimension on site plan."** Dimension to the nearest lot line for each exterior wall of the ADU.
- **"Provide rated assembly for the wall under 5 feet from lot line."** Specify the UL design and provide the section detail.
- **"Confirm openings comply with R302.1."** If the ADU has windows on a wall within 5 feet of the lot line, show the opening percentage of total wall area and confirm it falls under the cap.

## Fire-separation distance from the primary dwelling

R302.1 also applies between buildings on the same lot. For a detached ADU near the primary dwelling, the distance from the ADU's exterior wall to the primary dwelling's exterior wall affects the rating requirements on both walls.

In practice, Massachusetts ADU sites rarely place the detached ADU within 6 feet of the primary dwelling because of zoning setback requirements that bind tighter. But on tight urban parcels (Cambridge, Somerville, Boston), it can happen. When it does, expect a correction asking for both buildings' walls to have the appropriate rating.

## The MA amendments that matter

Massachusetts 780 CMR amendments to IRC 2021 modify some IRC R302 provisions. The most relevant for ADUs:

- **Sprinkler trigger** — The IRC 2021 baseline includes R313 (residential sprinklers required in new one- and two-family dwellings). Massachusetts has not adopted R313 for one- and two-family dwellings; sprinklers are not required by the state code for new ADUs. Some local bylaws have additional requirements; check for parcel-specific overlays.
- **Smoke and CO alarm placement** — IRC R314 (smoke alarms) and R315 (CO alarms) are adopted with MA amendments under MGL Ch 148 §§ 26F and 26F½. Hardwired with battery backup is the standard for new construction.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXX/Chapter148 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 148 §§ 26F (smoke) and 26F½ (CO) — MA alarm requirements]

## The response template for fire-separation corrections

When a plan checker writes a correction citing fire separation, the response is straightforward:

> "Item [#]: Fire-separation requirement at [location]. Per 780 CMR 10th Edition adopting IRC 2021 R302.[1 or 3], the assembly is detailed on Sheet [#] and consists of [UL design + materials]. Penetrations are firestopped per [listed system]. The assembly extends continuously per R302.[X.Y]. Please see the revised sheet for the updated detail."

Attach the revised sheet with the UL listing referenced explicitly. Most fire-separation corrections close on first revision once the listing is shown.

## Common contractor mistakes

- **Specifying an assembly without the UL listing.** Plan checkers want the listing reference. "1-hour fire-rated wall" is not enough; "1-hour fire-rated wall per UL U305" is.
- **Missing the firestop detail at penetrations.** Even a perfect wall assembly fails inspection if the dryer vent through the rated wall is not firestopped.
- **Treating R302.1 and R302.3 as the same rule.** They are different. Detached buildings near a lot line versus attached units within one building. Read the correction carefully and respond to the right rule.
- **Forgetting the assembly continuity check.** A wall that stops at the ceiling joist with no rated ceiling above is not a continuous separation.
