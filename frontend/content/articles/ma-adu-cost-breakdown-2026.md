# How Much Does an ADU Cost to Build in Massachusetts in 2026?

The most common question contractors get from a homeowner considering an ADU is "what will it cost." The honest answer is "between $200,000 and $500,000 depending on type, size, and city." This guide breaks down where each dollar goes, why Specialized Opt-In Code municipalities run higher than rural ones, and how to budget realistically rather than getting blindsided at certificate of occupancy.

## The headline ranges by ADU type

For a typical Massachusetts parcel in a covered city (Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline) in 2026:

- **Interior conversion ADU** (basement finish, attic conversion, garage conversion without footprint expansion): **$150,000 to $300,000**.
- **Attached new-construction ADU** (addition to the primary dwelling): **$250,000 to $450,000**.
- **Detached new-construction ADU** (separate structure on the same lot): **$300,000 to $550,000**.

These ranges are for the full package — design, permitting, construction, finishes, and energy code compliance. The lower end of each range applies to simpler, smaller projects with experienced builders. The upper end applies to complex sites, premium finishes, and tight schedules.

In non-Specialized-Code cities (most of suburban MA outside the inner ring), subtract roughly $10,000-$25,000 per project for the energy code premium difference.

## Construction — the dominant line item

For a 720 square-foot ADU (a common size — well below the 900 sqft state cap), construction runs the largest single cost:

- **Detached new construction**: $300-$500 per square foot in covered cities, so $216,000-$360,000 for the structure alone.
- **Attached new construction**: $250-$400 per square foot, so $180,000-$288,000.
- **Interior conversion**: $150-$280 per square foot, so $108,000-$202,000.

The drivers within construction:

- **Foundation work** — for detached ADUs, frost-depth footings (48 inches typical in MA per 780 CMR) plus the foundation wall plus slab. $30,000-$60,000.
- **Framing and shell** — walls, floor system, roof system. $50,000-$100,000 for a 720 sqft new-construction project.
- **Mechanical, plumbing, electrical** — heat pump, heat-pump water heater, electrical service or sub-panel, plumbing rough-in. $40,000-$80,000 in Specialized Code cities (where heat pumps and HPWH are required); $30,000-$60,000 elsewhere.
- **Insulation and air-sealing** — Specialized Code envelope is genuinely expensive. R-values plus continuous insulation plus blower-door target. $15,000-$30,000.
- **Drywall, finishes, kitchen, bath** — $40,000-$80,000 depending on finish level.
- **Site work, landscaping, exterior** — $15,000-$40,000.

## Permitting and city fees

The state floor under Chapter 150 of the Acts of 2024 caps what cities can require for ADU use, but it does not cap permit fees. Cities still set their own fee schedules.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150 §§ 7-8 — by-right ADU framework]

Typical MA permit fees for an ADU project:

- **Building permit fee**: $0.50-$2.00 per square foot of construction, sometimes with a flat administrative minimum. For a 720 sqft ADU, $360-$1,440.
- **Trade permit fees**: separate plumbing, gas, electrical, and HVAC permits each carry their own fees. $200-$800 each.
- **Plan review fee**: included in the building permit fee in most MA cities; some break it out.
- **Zoning review fee**: a few hundred dollars in most cities; can be more if special-permit relief is needed for non-ADU dimensional issues.
- **Historic district application fee** (if applicable): $100-$500 plus possible architectural drawing requirements.

Total permit fees typically: $1,500-$5,000 for a covered-city ADU. Higher in Boston with all sub-permits accounted for.

## Design and engineering fees

For a stamped permit application:

- **Architect**: $5,000-$25,000 for design through permit-set drawings, depending on project complexity. Some architects offer flat-fee ADU packages around $10,000-$15,000.
- **Structural engineer**: $1,500-$5,000 for a stamped structural design. Most ADUs need this, especially detached new construction.
- **HERS rater**: $1,500-$3,500 for design-phase consultation through final certification per master playbook §223 model-stage matching.
- **Civil engineer / site planner** (if needed): $2,000-$8,000 for a detailed site plan.
- **Surveyor**: $1,000-$3,000 for a property survey, often required by the city.

Total design and engineering: typically $10,000-$25,000 for a typical project; up to $40,000 for complex sites or historic districts.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXVI/Chapter112 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 112 §§ 60A and 81D — architect and engineer licensure requirements]

## Energy code premium — the Specialized Opt-In Code reality

For Specialized Opt-In Code cities (Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline plus ~60 others), the all-electric and high-performance envelope requirements add a meaningful premium:

- **Heat pump space conditioning** vs gas furnace: $3,000-$8,000 premium.
- **Heat-pump water heater** vs tankless gas: $1,500-$3,000 premium.
- **Higher-performance windows** (U-0.30 or better): $1,000-$3,000 premium.
- **Continuous wall insulation** (vs cavity-only): $2,000-$5,000.
- **Blower-door target compliance** (3 ACH50 or better air-sealing): $500-$2,000 in materials and labor.
- **HERS rater fee**: $1,500-$3,500 (also listed under design).

Total Specialized Code premium: $10,000-$25,000 over Base Code. This is the cost of the energy code, separate from the cost of the ADU. Plan for it.

## Financing options

Most homeowners do not pay cash for an ADU. The realistic financing options:

- **Cash-out refinance** of the primary mortgage. Adds the ADU cost to the existing mortgage. Requires sufficient home equity. Interest rates apply to the full mortgage, not just the new portion.
- **Home equity loan or HELOC**. Second mortgage backed by the equity in the primary. Typically 80% LTV combined cap. Faster than refi but typically higher rate.
- **Renovation construction loan** (Fannie Mae HomeStyle, FHA 203k). Construction-period loan that converts to permanent. More paperwork but covers the build-out period.
- **MassHousing ADU loan products** — Massachusetts has launched specific ADU financing programs. Worth checking the current MassHousing offerings.
- **Rental income consideration in underwriting** — some lenders count anticipated ADU rental income toward debt-to-income calculations, which can stretch borrowing capacity.

A realistic total cost of capital for a $350,000 ADU project at current rates: around $1,500-$2,500 monthly debt service over 30 years, depending on equity and loan structure.

## Construction timeline implications

An ADU project takes 6 to 18 months from initial design through certificate of occupancy:

- **Design phase**: 2-4 months
- **Permit review**: 1-3 months (longer in historic districts under MGL Ch 40C)
- **Construction**: 4-9 months for new construction, 3-6 for conversion
- **Final inspections and HERS certification**: 2-6 weeks

Time is itself a cost. Carrying costs (loan interest during construction, property tax on the parcel, insurance) add up. A realistic carrying-cost line item: $5,000-$15,000 over the construction period for most MA ADU projects.

For a 720 sqft detached ADU in a covered MA city in 2026:

| Line item | Low | High |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Construction | $216,000 | $360,000 |
| Permit fees | $1,500 | $5,000 |
| Design + engineering + HERS | $10,000 | $25,000 |
| Specialized Code premium (already in construction figure for covered cities) | — | — |
| Carrying costs during construction | $5,000 | $15,000 |
| Contingency (10-15%) | $25,000 | $60,000 |
| **Total** | **$257,500** | **$465,000** |

For a 720 sqft attached ADU: subtract roughly $50,000-$80,000 from each end of the range.

For a 720 sqft interior conversion: subtract roughly $100,000-$150,000.

## What this guide does not cover

- **Tax implications.** ADU income is taxable. Property tax may increase based on the assessed value of the new dwelling unit. Consult a CPA.
- **Insurance changes.** Adding an ADU changes homeowners insurance — specifically the dwelling coverage, often to a multi-family policy. Get a quote before construction.
- **Regional cost variations within MA.** Western MA cities have meaningfully lower construction costs than the inner-ring suburbs. Subtract roughly 15-25% from the figures above for projects in Western MA.
- **Custom features.** High-end finishes, smart-home integration, accessibility features beyond minimum, premium landscaping all add up. Plan for them line-by-line in the budget rather than treating them as part of the base figure.
