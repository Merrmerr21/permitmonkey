# Somerville ADU 2026 Contractor Guide

Somerville is a particularly interesting case for ADU work after Chapter 150. The city adopted a form-based zoning ordinance — the Somerville Zoning Ordinance, or SZO — that emphasizes building form, frontage type, and neighborhood character rather than the use-table approach most Massachusetts municipalities still rely on. State use-preemption maps onto a form-based code differently than onto a traditional code.

This guide walks through what an ADU contractor needs to know about Somerville specifically: the SZO bind points, the historic district overlays, the Specialized Energy Code, and the response pattern when a Somerville plan checker invokes a preempted requirement.

## The state floor still applies in Somerville

The same three citations govern any Somerville ADU permit:

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40A/Section3 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 40A § 3 — by-right ADU paragraph after Ch 150 § 8]

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150 §§ 7-8 — Affordable Homes Act ADU provisions]

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/accessory-dwelling-units | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 760 CMR 71.00 — EOHLC implementing regulation]

The state floor is identical: lesser of 900 sqft or 50% of primary; no owner-occupancy; one parking space max, zero within 0.5 miles of qualifying transit (which in Somerville is most of the city — every Red Line stop, the Green Line Extension stations from Union Square through Tufts, multiple key bus stations).

## How the SZO form-based code interacts with state use protection

The SZO uses transect zones, not use districts. A parcel sits in a zone like Neighborhood Residence (NR), Urban Residence (UR), or one of the mixed-use zones. Each zone defines a building envelope (frontage, setback, height, lot coverage, FAR) and a permitted-form list (single-family, two-family, townhouse, mid-rise mixed use).

State use-preemption gives ADUs a protected use status. But the SZO's form rules — the building envelope — are FORM, not USE. So:

- **State law** says you can put an ADU on a Somerville single-family parcel. By-right.
- **SZO** says the combined building footprint cannot exceed the lot coverage maximum, the combined FAR cannot exceed the zone maximum, and the structure must respect the prescribed setbacks and height.

These two rules coexist. The fight is at the edges — when a Somerville requirement is so restrictive in practice that it functionally bans the use, courts and the EOHLC have signaled that crosses into preemption.

[source: https://www.somervillema.gov/departments/planning-and-zoning | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: City of Somerville Planning and Zoning — SZO administration]

## FAR is usually the binding constraint

In NR and UR zones, FAR runs 0.5 to 1.0 typical. With an existing primary at FAR 0.6, an ADU at 720 sqft on a 5,000-sqft lot adds 0.144 to the ratio — pushing combined FAR to 0.744 and consuming a meaningful portion of the headroom. Plan ahead. Calculate FAR on the cover sheet in plain numbers; show primary + ADU + total against the district cap.

Lot coverage runs 35-50% in most residential zones, less restrictive than FAR for typical ADU-sized additions but still worth confirming.

## Historic district overlays cover significant ground

Somerville has multiple local historic districts under MGL Ch 40C, administered by the Somerville Historic Preservation Commission:

- Old West Somerville
- Spring Hill
- Westwood Road
- Several individual landmarks

If your parcel sits within one of these — confirm via the city's GIS layer — Historic Preservation Commission review applies on top of zoning. Like Cambridge, this authority is **not** preempted by Chapter 150.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40C | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 40C — Historic Districts Act, separate authority not preempted]

The Commission reviews exterior changes visible from a public way. A detached ADU is squarely in scope. An interior conversion ADU with no exterior alteration is generally not. Add 8-16 weeks to the timeline if review applies.

## Specialized Opt-In Energy Code adopted

Somerville is on the Specialized Code per 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC. The compliance path is the same as Cambridge or Boston: all-electric (or near-zero fossil fuel) HVAC, heat-pump water heater or equivalent, high-performance envelope, HERS rating in the 45-55 range.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/specialized-stretch-code | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC — Specialized Opt-In Energy Code]

Engage a HERS rater at design phase. Show the compliance path and identified rater on the cover sheet. Missing energy compliance documentation is the most common Somerville correction after dimensional issues.

## When Somerville requires conditions preempted by state law

The same response template works:

> "The proposed ADU is a Protected Use under MGL Ch 40A § 3 (as amended by St. 2024, c. 150, § 8) and 760 CMR 71.00. The [parking / owner-occupancy / special-permit] requirement at SZO [section] is preempted by state law to the extent it conflicts with the protected-use framework. We respectfully request the comment be withdrawn or revised."

Somerville Planning and Zoning has been aware of the preemption since EOHLC published its guidance. Most reviewers have caught up. If a reviewer cites an SZO section to require something the state floor protects against, ask for the specific subsection — and confirm whether the SZO has been updated to align with Chapter 150. Many older SZO sections that conflict have been functionally superseded but not yet redlined out of the published text.

## What good Somerville ADU plan packages include

- **Cover sheet declaration** referencing MGL Ch 40A § 3 protected use.
- **FAR and lot coverage calculation** as a clean table showing primary + ADU + combined vs the SZO maximum for the parcel's zone.
- **Building form characterization** under the SZO transect zone, not just use. The form-based code expects you to show how the proposed structure fits the zone's frontage and envelope.
- **Historic district status** on the cover sheet, with parcel ID confirmation if outside any district.
- **Energy compliance path** — Specialized Opt-In Code, HERS rater identified by name, target rating.
- **MBTA proximity declaration** if within 0.5 miles of a Red Line, Green Line Extension, or qualifying bus station — invokes the parking exemption under 760 CMR 71.00.

## Common Somerville-specific gotchas

- **Two-family parcels** — A parcel zoned for two-family is **not** automatically eligible for an ADU under the state floor; the protected use applies to single-family parcels in single-family-zoned districts. If your two-family parcel is in a zone that allows only single-family by-right, the analysis is different and may require a special permit or amendment.
- **Combined dwellings counted** — When the SZO calculates combined FAR, the existing primary plus the proposed ADU both count. There is no implicit FAR exemption for the ADU.
- **Older SZO sections lingering** — If your reviewer cites an SZO section that pre-dates Chapter 150, check whether the cited section has been amended. The municipal code may not have been redlined to remove conflicts; the actual operative rule is the state floor.
