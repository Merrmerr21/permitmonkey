# Newton ADU 2026 Contractor Guide

Newton is a different ADU calculus than Cambridge or Somerville. The city's residential districts run to large minimum lot sizes — SR-1 minimums often exceed 25,000 square feet — and most parcels comfortably accommodate a primary plus an ADU within the dimensional envelope. The bind point in Newton is rarely lot coverage. It is design review and historic district overlays.

This guide walks through the Newton dimensional framework, the Historic District Commission process, the Specialized Opt-In Code, and the response template when a Newton requirement crosses the state-floor line.

## The state floor that applies to every Newton parcel

Three citations govern any Newton ADU permit:

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40A/Section3 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 40A § 3 — by-right ADU paragraph after Ch 150 § 8]

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150 §§ 7-8 — Affordable Homes Act ADU provisions]

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/accessory-dwelling-units | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 760 CMR 71.00 — EOHLC implementing regulation]

Lesser of 900 sqft or 50% of primary as the size cap. No owner-occupancy requirement. One parking space max, zero within 0.5 miles of qualifying transit. In Newton, the qualifying transit is primarily the Green Line (D branch — Newton Highlands, Newton Centre, Newton Highlands, Newton Center, etc.), commuter rail (Newton Corner, West Newton, Newtonville, Auburndale on the Worcester Line), and key MBTA bus station designations.

## Newton dimensional standards — the SR districts

Newton's residential districts are SR-1, SR-2, SR-3, and a few smaller MR (multi-residence) zones. Single-family residential on most parcels is one of the SR-N variants.

The SR districts are dimensionally generous compared to other inner-ring suburbs, but the substantial minimum lot sizes mean ADU placement faces different constraints:

- **SR-1** — minimum lot ~25,000 sqft, typical setbacks 30 ft front / 20 ft side / 30 ft rear. Front setback often binds.
- **SR-2** — minimum lot ~10,000 sqft, setbacks tighter than SR-1.
- **SR-3** — minimum lot ~7,000 sqft, setbacks tighter still.

Floor area ratio caps run roughly 0.20-0.50 depending on district. With a substantial primary already on the parcel, FAR can become the binding constraint even on a large lot.

[source: https://www.newtonma.gov/government/planning | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: City of Newton Planning Department — zoning bylaw and ADU amendments]

State law preempts the by-right protected use. It does not preempt the SR dimensional rules. Plan accordingly: lot coverage and FAR calculations belong on the cover sheet for any Newton ADU submittal.

## Historic District Commission overlays — the Newton wildcard

Newton has multiple local historic districts under MGL Ch 40C, administered by the Newton Historical Commission:

- Newton Centre
- Chestnut Hill (partial)
- Newtonville
- Auburndale
- West Newton Hill
- Multiple individual landmarks

If your parcel sits within one of these, the Historical Commission process applies on top of zoning. Like Cambridge and Somerville, this authority is **not** preempted by the state ADU law.

[source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40C | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: MGL Ch 40C — Historic Districts Act, separate authority not preempted]

What matters in practice:

- The Commission reviews exterior alterations only. Interior conversion ADUs without exterior changes typically sit outside their jurisdiction.
- A detached new-construction ADU in any of these districts is squarely in scope and faces a 60-120 day Commission review.
- The Commission's standards are stricter than zoning for materials, massing, and visible architectural detail. Plan for compliance with the district's character study, not just zoning dimensions.

## Specialized Opt-In Energy Code adopted

Newton has adopted the Specialized Opt-In Code under 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC. Same compliance path as Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Brookline.

[source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/specialized-stretch-code | retrieved: 2026-05-07 | citation: 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC — Specialized Opt-In Energy Code adopted by Newton]

Compliance mechanics:

- All-electric or near-zero fossil fuel for new construction. Heat pump for space conditioning. Heat-pump water heater or equivalent.
- High-performance envelope. Wall R-21 to R-30 plus continuous insulation, ceiling R-49 minimum, foundation R-15 minimum, U-factor 0.30 windows or better.
- HERS rating in the 45-55 range typical. Engage a HERS rater at design phase.

Newton Building Department flags missing energy compliance documentation as a routine correction. Show the path on the cover sheet.

## When Newton requires conditions preempted by state law

The standard preemption response works in Newton:

> "The proposed ADU is a Protected Use under MGL Ch 40A § 3 (as amended by St. 2024, c. 150, § 8) and 760 CMR 71.00. The [parking / owner-occupancy / special-permit] requirement at [Newton bylaw section] is preempted by state law to the extent it conflicts with the protected-use framework. We respectfully request the comment be withdrawn or revised."

Newton has been responsive on preemption issues. Most plan checkers acknowledge the citation and revise.

The one Newton-specific subtlety: the Historical Commission process is **not** preempted. If a Newton correction routes through the Commission and the Commission denies on aesthetic grounds, the Ch 150 preemption argument does not apply. Different statutory authority, different appeal path.

## What good Newton ADU plan packages include

- Cover sheet protected-use declaration referencing MGL Ch 40A § 3.
- Code summary table: 780 CMR 10th Edition, 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC (Specialized), 248 CMR (plumbing), 527 CMR 12 (electrical).
- Lot coverage and FAR calculation as a clean table — primary + ADU + combined vs SR district maximum.
- Setback dimensions on all four sides, with a clean tie to the SR district standards.
- Historic district status declared. If outside any district, confirm by parcel ID against the city's GIS layer.
- Energy compliance path — Specialized Opt-In Code, HERS rater identified by name, target rating.
- For detached ADUs in historic districts: pre-submittal coordination with the Historical Commission. The Commission's design preferences are not always intuitive; a 30-minute design review meeting before drawings are finalized saves weeks downstream.

## Common Newton-specific gotchas

- **Aesthetic regulation through historic district** — Newton's Historical Commissions can block design choices that comply with zoning. Materials, fenestration, massing, roof pitch all face stricter scrutiny than in non-district parcels. This is legitimate authority, not preempted.
- **Combined FAR with substantial existing primaries** — Newton's housing stock includes many large existing homes. FAR is a binding constraint surprisingly often even on 15,000-square-foot lots.
- **Limited transit coverage outside the Green Line corridor** — most of Newton's neighborhoods do not qualify for the parking exemption. Plan for one space unless your specific parcel is within 0.5 miles of a Green Line stop, commuter rail station, or qualifying bus station.
- **Conservation Commission** — Newton has substantial wetland resources. Parcels near rivers, ponds, or wet areas trigger conservation review under MGL Ch 131 § 40 separately from zoning.
