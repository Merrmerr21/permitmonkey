## Zoning Framework

Brookline's Zoning By-Law structures residential use across several single-family and two-family districts:

- **Single Residence A (S-A)** — largest lot / most restrictive
- **Single Residence B (S-B)**
- **Single Residence C (S-C)**
- **Single Residence D (S-D)** — smallest single-family
- **Two-Family (T-5, T-6)** — two-family zones
- **Multi-Family (M-1.0, M-1.5, M-2.0, M-2.5)** — multi-family
- **Local and General Business** — commercial

ADU by-right provisions under Ch 150 of 2024 apply in the S-A through S-D districts.

## ADU Provisions

Brookline's Zoning By-Law Section 4.07 (typical reference — verify via eCode360) addresses accessory dwelling units. Post-Ch 150 updates expected.

Post-Ch 150 compliance:
- ADUs allowed by-right in Single Residence districts consistent with state law
- Size per state (900 sq ft or 50% of primary, lesser)
- No owner-occupancy requirement
- Parking per state (Brookline Village, Brookline Hills, Chestnut Hill, Coolidge Corner, and Washington Square are all near Green Line Line C/D/E; Longwood is near D Line; almost all of Brookline is within 0.5 mi of transit)

## Dimensional Standards (Typical)

Brookline's dimensional standards are strict in some districts. Approximate:

**Single Residence A (S-A):**
- Min lot area: 10,000+ sq ft
- Front setback: 30 ft
- Side setback: 20 ft
- Rear setback: 30 ft
- Max height: 35 ft
- Lot coverage: typically 20–25%

**Single Residence B (S-B):**
- Min lot area: 7,500 sq ft
- Side setback: 15 ft
- Max height: 35 ft
- Lot coverage: typically 25%

**Single Residence C (S-C):**
- Min lot area: 5,000 sq ft
- Side setback: 10 ft
- Max height: 35 ft
- Lot coverage: typically 30%

**Single Residence D (S-D):**
- Min lot area: 4,000 sq ft
- Side setback: 7.5 ft
- Max height: 35 ft
- Lot coverage: typically 35%

**Accessory structure setbacks** — Brookline's accessory structure rules are generally stricter than some MA towns. Verify per-district.

## Design Standards

Brookline applies design standards consistent with historic character of many neighborhoods:
- Materials consistent with neighborhood vernacular
- Window placement for privacy
- Roofline scale
- Landscape buffering may be required

No discretionary design board review for typical by-right ADUs, but design standards are meaningful.

## Historic Districts

Brookline has multiple locally-designated historic districts:
- Pill Hill Local Historic District
- Graffam-McKay Local Historic District
- Cottage Street Local Historic District
- And others

Exterior modifications in these districts require Preservation Commission review. Brookline also has a neighborhood conservation district program.

## Stretch / Specialized Energy Code

Brookline has adopted the **Specialized Opt-In Code**. Standard requirements apply.

## Short-Term Rental

Brookline has STR regulations. Most STR use is restricted in residential zones. Verify current before designing for STR use.

## Submittal Requirements (Typical)

- Building permit application
- Site plan with dimensional compliance demonstration
- Floor plans, elevations, sections
- Specialized Code energy compliance documentation
- Structural / M/P/E coordination
- Preservation Commission review if in historic district
- Conservation Commission filing if within 100 ft of wetland

## Fees

Brookline permit fees follow standard MA pattern. Verify current fee schedule with Building Department.

## Review Timeline

- Administrative by-right ADU: 3–6 weeks typical
- With Preservation Commission review: add 4–6 weeks

## Known Gotchas for Brookline ADU Permits

1. **Small lots in S-C and S-D** — fitting a compliant ADU within dimensional bylaws can be tough
2. **Tree preservation** — Brookline has active tree preservation standards
3. **Historic / neighborhood conservation districts** — many neighborhoods fall within overlay review
4. **Preservation Commission's reputation** — Brookline Preservation is known for thorough design review; allow extra time
5. **Specialized Energy Code** — full compliance required

## Preemption Watch Items

Brookline's zoning by-law may have legacy owner-occupancy provisions requiring update. Watch for:
- Accessory apartment provisions pre-dating Ch 150 with discretionary review
- Parking requirements exceeding state floor
- Design-standard language that becomes effectively discretionary
