# Boston — MA ADU City Reference

## Key Entities

- **Boston Planning & Development Agency (BPDA)** — planning, design review, historic districts, Article 80 design review for larger projects
- **Inspectional Services Department (ISD)** — building permits, code compliance, inspections
- **Zoning Board of Appeal (ZBA)** — variances, appeals
- **Boston Landmarks Commission** — historic districts (Beacon Hill, Back Bay, South End, Fort Point Channel, Mission Hill Triangle, etc.)
- **Boston Conservation Commission** — wetlands

## Zoning Framework

Boston has a district-based zoning code with neighborhood-specific articles (Article 51 is Brighton, Article 56 is Hyde Park, etc.). ADU rules may vary by neighborhood article. As of 2025, Boston updated zoning to comply with Ch 150 of the Acts of 2024.

## ADU Provisions (Post-Ch 150 Compliance)

Boston has adopted ADU-specific amendments to the zoning code. Key provisions include:

- ADUs allowed by-right in residential zoning districts consistent with state law
- Size limit per state: 900 sq ft or 50% of primary (lesser)
- No owner-occupancy requirement
- Parking per state (max 1, zero if within 0.5 mi of transit — Boston has extensive MBTA coverage so most lots qualify for zero)
- Design review may apply in certain districts and overlays

**Verification note:** Boston's ADU zoning implementation is actively evolving. Verify specific Article text per project at https://www.boston.gov/departments/boston-planning-development-agency/zoning-code-and-maps before citing.

## Dimensional Standards (Typical)

Dimensional standards vary by zoning sub-district. Common Boston residential districts:
- **R1 / 1F** — single-family
- **R2 / 2F** — two-family
- **R3 / 3F** — three-family
- Neighborhood-specific sub-districts with specific height and FAR rules

**Typical single-family dimensional standards:**
- Front setback: varies 10-25 ft
- Side setback: varies 5-10 ft
- Rear setback: varies 20-30 ft
- Height: 35 ft typical but varies
- Lot coverage / FAR: varies by sub-district

**Gotcha:** Boston is one of the densest MA cities. Many urban lots cannot physically fit a detached ADU within setback rules. Conversion ADUs (basement, attic, garage) are far more common.

## Design Review

- Article 80 design review applies to larger projects, NOT typical single ADUs
- Neighborhood-specific design guidelines may apply
- Historic districts require separate Landmarks Commission review

## Historic Districts

Boston has multiple locally-designated historic districts. If the property is in one:
- Landmarks Commission review required for exterior modifications
- Process: 4-8 weeks
- Review based on historic district design guidelines (vary by district)

Major Boston historic districts:
- Beacon Hill
- Back Bay
- South End
- Bay Village
- Fort Point Channel
- Mission Hill Triangle
- St. Botolph
- Others — see Landmarks Commission map

## Stretch / Specialized Energy Code

Boston has adopted the **Specialized Opt-In Code**. New construction and substantial renovation must meet net-zero or near-net-zero performance.

Implications for ADUs:
- All-electric heating (heat pumps) or renewable offset
- Higher envelope R-values
- Solar-ready provisions
- HERS rating typically 45-55

## Short-Term Rental

Boston has a licensing regime for short-term rentals. ADU used for STR must comply:
- Owner-adjacent STR requires owner registration
- Short-term Rental ordinance (Sec. 9-14) governs
- Some districts prohibit STR in residential zones

Source: https://www.boston.gov/departments/inspectional-services/short-term-rentals

## Wetlands

Boston Conservation Commission jurisdiction over wetlands resource areas under MGL Ch 131 §40. Filing required within 100 ft of wetland resource area.

## Submittal Requirements (ADU, Typical)

- Completed building permit application
- Site plan
- Floor plans, elevations, sections
- Structural calculations if required
- Energy code compliance documentation (HERS rating or equivalent)
- Fire separation detail
- Emergency egress compliance
- Owner authorization (if applicant isn't owner)
- Contractor license verification
- Workers comp certification

## Fees (Approximate)

Permit fees are calculated on construction valuation. Typical single-family ADU (~$100-200K valuation):
- Building permit: ~$1,500-3,000
- Mechanical / plumbing / electrical permits: separate, ~$200-500 each
- Plan review may have additional fees

Verify current fee schedule at ISD.

## Review Timeline (Typical)

- Administrative building permit: 2-6 weeks if complete submittal and by-right
- With Landmarks Commission review: add 6-10 weeks
- With Conservation Commission review: add 8-12 weeks

## Known Gotchas for Boston ADU Permits

1. **Lot coverage limits often block detached ADUs on small urban lots** — conversion is usually the path
2. **Historic districts are wide** — many desirable ADU sites fall within Landmarks jurisdiction
3. **Parking** — while state preempts within 0.5 mi transit, be precise with measurement; many Boston lots qualify
4. **Existing non-conforming structures** — primary dwelling may already be non-conforming (built before current zoning); adding ADU interacts with non-conforming use rules
5. **Condo vs. rental** — creating a separately-marketable ADU unit in a condo building has separate condo declaration implications
6. **Article 80** — large projects trigger Article 80 review, but single-ADU projects generally do not

## Preemption Watch Items

Potential conflicts with Ch 150 preemption to watch for in Boston corrections:
- Any owner-occupancy reference from older bylaw text
- Any "accessory apartment" (old terminology) review process that requires ZBA approval for a single ADU
- Parking requirements >1 space in non-transit-exempt locations

If encountered, invoke preemption per `conflicts-and-preemption.md` in the `massachusetts-adu` skill.
