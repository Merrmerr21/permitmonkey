# Chapter 150 of the Acts of 2024 — The Affordable Homes Act

**Official citation:** St. 2024, c. 150
**Signed:** August 6, 2024 by Governor Maura Healey
**Effective (ADU provisions):** February 2, 2025
**Source:** https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150

## Sections Relevant to ADUs

### Section 7 — Amends MGL Ch 40A § 1A (Definitions)

Adds the definition of "accessory dwelling unit" to the zoning act's definitions section. This is the legal definition that every municipal bylaw must now work from.

**Key definitional language:**

> "Accessory dwelling unit", a self-contained housing unit, inclusive of sleeping, cooking and sanitary facilities on the same lot as a principal dwelling, subject to otherwise applicable dimensional and parking requirements, that: (i) maintains a separate entrance, either directly from the outside or through an entry hall or corridor shared with the principal dwelling sufficient to meet the requirements of the state building code for safe egress; (ii) is not larger in gross floor area than ½ the gross floor area of the principal dwelling or 900 square feet, whichever is smaller; and (iii) is subject to such additional restrictions as may be imposed by a municipality including, but not limited to, additional size restrictions and restrictions or prohibitions on short-term rental.

**Engineering annotations:**

- "Self-contained" means sleeping, cooking, AND sanitary — all three. Missing any one makes it not an ADU under state law (and likely triggers different rules).
- "Same lot as a principal dwelling" — ADUs must be accessory to an existing principal dwelling. No ADU without a primary.
- "Separate entrance" can be direct or through a shared hall/corridor that meets building code egress. Important: this is not a state authority over city design rules on entrance LOCATION — just a minimum egress baseline.
- Size = **½ gross floor area of primary OR 900 sq ft, whichever is smaller**. Note the "smaller" — it's a ceiling, not a floor. A 1,200 sq ft primary house caps the ADU at 600 sq ft (½ of primary), not 900.
- Municipalities CAN impose "additional size restrictions" below the state ceiling and CAN prohibit short-term rental. This is key local authority.

### Section 8 — Amends MGL Ch 40A § 3 (Uses Permitted)

Adds the operative by-right paragraph making ADUs a protected use. This is the preemption teeth.

**Key operative language:**

> "No zoning ordinance or by-law shall prohibit, unreasonably restrict or require a special permit or other discretionary zoning approval for the use of land or structures for a single accessory dwelling unit, or the rental thereof, in a single-family residential zoning district; provided, that the use may be subject to reasonable regulations, including site plan review, regulations concerning dimensional setbacks and the bulk and height of structures and may be subject to restrictions and prohibitions on short-term rental."

**Engineering annotations:**

- "Shall not prohibit" — full prohibition is out
- "Unreasonably restrict" — standard of review on municipal rules is "reasonableness"; legal gray area
- "Require a special permit or other discretionary zoning approval" — BY-RIGHT. No ZBA hearing. No variance. Administrative review only.
- "In a single-family residential zoning district" — the preemption applies only in single-family zones. Multi-family zones and mixed-use zones are outside this provision (but cities often also allow ADUs there independently).
- "Single accessory dwelling unit, OR the rental thereof" — includes the rental, so cities can't ban rental of a by-right ADU (except short-term, which is explicitly permitted to regulate).
- "Reasonable regulations, including site plan review" — site plan review is allowed. ZBA hearing is not.
- "Dimensional setbacks and the bulk and height of structures" — locality retains these. This is where most of the real design friction will be.
- "May be subject to restrictions and prohibitions on short-term rental" — explicit grant of authority to cities to regulate or ban STR use of ADUs.

**Owner-occupancy paragraph (follows):**

> "The use of land or structures for an accessory dwelling unit under this paragraph shall not require owner occupancy of either the accessory dwelling unit or the principal dwelling; provided, that not more than 1 additional parking space shall be required for an accessory dwelling unit; and provided further, that no additional parking space shall be required for an accessory dwelling located not more than 0.5 miles from a commuter rail station, subway station, ferry terminal or bus station."

**Engineering annotations:**

- **No owner-occupancy requirement allowed.** This is a flat preemption. Any city bylaw requiring the owner to live on-site (in either the ADU or the primary) is unenforceable.
- **Max 1 additional parking space.** State ceiling on parking.
- **Zero additional parking if within 0.5 mi of commuter rail, subway, ferry terminal, or bus station.** Note: "bus station" is arguably broader than "bus stop" — practitioners disagree on whether a standard MBTA local bus stop counts. Conservative read: only designated bus terminals count. Aggressive read: any bus stop with regular service. This is an open interpretation question.

### Multiple ADUs (more than one per lot)

Multiple ADUs on one lot are NOT by-right under state law. They still require a special permit per the general rules in MGL Ch 40A. The state-level preemption only protects a **single** ADU per lot.

## What Ch 150 §§7-8 Did NOT Do

- Did NOT preempt local dimensional setbacks, bulk, height
- Did NOT preempt local site plan review (allowed explicitly)
- Did NOT override 780 CMR (State Building Code) requirements
- Did NOT override local historic district or wetlands protections
- Did NOT create a state ADU permit (still issued at local level)
- Did NOT preempt local authority over additional ADUs (more than one per lot still requires special permit)
- Did NOT override 521 CMR (accessibility) or 527 CMR (fire) standards
- Did NOT create a fee schedule or limit local fees

## Interaction with 760 CMR 71.00

EOHLC issued implementing regulations in 760 CMR 71.00 "Protected Use Accessory Dwelling Units" to operationalize Ch 150. The regulation fills in definitional gaps and provides the "Protected Use ADU" framework. See `760-cmr-71-protected-use-adu.md`.
