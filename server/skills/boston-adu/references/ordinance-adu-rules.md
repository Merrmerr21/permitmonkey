# Boston ADU Operational Rules

This file covers operational rules — who can live in the ADU, who can rent it, when it can be a short-term rental, whether it can be sold separately. Dimensional rules (size, height, setbacks) are in `ordinance-adu-standards.md`. Zoning framework and neighborhood-by-neighborhood rollout are in `zoning-residential.md`.

## State-Law Floor on Operational Rules (Applies to Most MA, Boston Caveat)

For municipalities zoned under MGL Ch 40A, Ch. 150 of the Acts of 2024 (Affordable Homes Act, §§7-8) sets a floor on operational rules that cities cannot regulate below:

- **No owner-occupancy requirement.** Cities cannot require the property owner to occupy either the primary dwelling or the ADU. [source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-04-22 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150, §8 amending MGL Ch 40A §3 — see `massachusetts-adu` skill `chapter-150-of-2024.md` for full text]
- **No first-ADU special permit.** ADUs are by-right in single-family residential districts; cities cannot require a special permit for the first ADU on a lot. [source: ibid.]
- **Parking maximum 1 space; 0 within 0.5 mi of MBTA station / commuter rail / ferry / subway / bus station.** [source: ibid.]
- **Short-term rental of an ADU may be locally restricted or prohibited.** State law explicitly preserves municipal authority on STR. [source: ibid.]
- **Sale of an ADU separate from the primary dwelling is generally not permitted under MA law** unless a separate condominium declaration is filed (see condo-conversion analysis in the `massachusetts-adu` skill).

**Boston caveat:** Boston operates under a special-act zoning enabling framework. Ch. 150 amended Ch 40A, but Boston's relationship to Ch 40A is not standard. Whether each Ch. 150 floor item directly governs Boston requires case-by-case preemption analysis — see `eohlc-compliance.md` (forthcoming) and the `conflicts-and-preemption.md` reference in the `massachusetts-adu` skill before asserting state preemption against a Boston correction.

[source: https://www.bostonplans.org/planning-zoning/zoning-initiatives/neighborhood-housing | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA's framing that Boston's response to Ch. 150 requires its own zoning amendments rather than automatic flow-through]

## Owner Occupancy

**Per the state-law floor (and consistent with Boston practice for amended neighborhoods):** owner occupancy of either the primary dwelling or the ADU cannot be required as a condition of the ADU permit. [source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/SessionLaws/Acts/2024/Chapter150 | retrieved: 2026-04-22 | citation: St. 2024, c. 150, §8]

For Boston neighborhoods not yet amended (Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park drafts pending; all other neighborhoods governed by pre-Ch. 150 articles), some pre-existing article text may still reference owner-occupancy or "accessory apartment" approval through the Zoning Board of Appeal (ZBA). When such language appears in a correction letter, **flag it as a potential preemption issue** rather than treating it as a binding requirement. The contractor's response should cite the state-law floor and request the city remove the owner-occupancy condition; escalate to ZBA hearing only if the city refuses.

## Long-Term Rental

Long-term rental of a Boston ADU (≥30 days per occupancy) is allowed under the state-law floor and not subject to special Boston restrictions beyond the underlying zoning approval and any deed restrictions on the parcel. Standard MA landlord-tenant law (MGL Ch 186) governs the lease.

The Mattapan amendment (PLAN: Mattapan, adopted February 2024) explicitly enables long-term-rental ADUs as part of its by-right small-scale residential framework. **Quote of the amendment text TBD pending Municode access** — the dimensional and operational specifics of the Mattapan ADU rule must be read from the controlling Article on Municode (https://library.municode.com/ma/boston/codes/redevelopment_authority). [source: https://www.bostonplans.org/planning-zoning/zoning-initiatives/neighborhood-housing | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA Neighborhood Housing Zoning page confirms Mattapan adoption with by-right outcome]

## Short-Term Rental (Sec. 9-14)

Boston regulates short-term rentals under **City of Boston Code, Sec. 9-14** (the Short-Term Rental Ordinance). Use of an ADU as a short-term rental triggers Sec. 9-14 in addition to the underlying zoning approval.

[source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\.claude\skills\ma-city-research\references\boston.md (last_verified 2026-04-22) | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: existing PermitMonkey research notes confirm Sec. 9-14 governs Boston STR]

[source: https://www.boston.gov/departments/inspectional-services/short-term-rentals | retrieved: 2026-04-22 | citation: ISD STR program page (verified in earlier research; URL may have changed by 2026-05-03 — re-verify before citing in contractor response)]

**Key Sec. 9-14 features (per existing research):**

- Owner registration required for owner-adjacent STR
- Some districts prohibit STR in residential zones
- The state-law floor under MGL Ch 40A §3 (as amended) **explicitly preserves municipal authority to restrict or prohibit STR of ADUs** — Sec. 9-14 is not preempted by Ch. 150

## Sale and Subdivision

Under MA law generally, an ADU cannot be sold separately from the primary dwelling unless a condominium declaration is filed under MGL Ch 183A. This is a state-level rule and applies in Boston.

[source: MGL Ch 183A | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: MA condominium statute]

## Deed Restrictions

For Boston neighborhoods with completed amendments, the BPDA's published framework does not appear to require deed restrictions tied to the ADU permit (i.e., no recorded affordability covenant as a condition of approval). This is consistent with the state-law floor, which does not require deed restrictions for ADUs that are not part of an inclusionary or affordable-housing program.

For pre-amendment neighborhoods, some pre-existing article text or ZBA variance conditions may reference deed restrictions; treat these the same way as owner-occupancy language (flag as potential preemption issue if state-law analysis applies).

[source: https://www.bostonplans.org/planning-zoning/zoning-initiatives/neighborhood-housing | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA Neighborhood Housing Zoning page, no deed-restriction language in published outcomes]

## Number of ADUs Per Lot

The state-law floor under Ch. 150 establishes by-right entitlement for one ADU per lot in single-family residential districts. Whether a Boston neighborhood permits more than one ADU on a lot is a question of the neighborhood-specific Article post-amendment.

## Conversion vs. New Construction

Boston ADU practice strongly favors conversion ADUs (basement, attic, garage) over detached new-construction ADUs because of dense lot constraints. The operational rules above apply equally to conversion and new-construction ADUs unless the neighborhood-specific Article carves out a distinction.

## Workflow for the Agent

When a Boston ADU correction touches operational rules:

1. **Identify the neighborhood and the controlling Article.** Use the Boston Zoning Viewer (https://maps.bostonplans.org/zoningviewer/) for the parcel.
2. **Determine amendment status** from `zoning-residential.md`'s implementation table (Mattapan adopted Feb 2024; Roslindale / West Roxbury / Hyde Park in progress; all others pre-Ch. 150).
3. **For owner-occupancy, parking, special-permit, or first-ADU language in the correction:** invoke state-law preemption analysis if the parcel is in a non-amended neighborhood AND state law applies to Boston for the specific question (Ch 40A reach is the gating issue — see `eohlc-compliance.md`).
4. **For STR, sale, deed-restriction questions:** Boston's authority is preserved; do not assert preemption.
5. **Quote the specific Article section number and Municode URL with retrieval date in the contractor response.** Do not paraphrase a Boston ordinance section without quoting it.
