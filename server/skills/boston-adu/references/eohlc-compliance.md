# 760 CMR 71.00 Application in Boston

## The Open Legal Question This File Documents

For most Massachusetts municipalities, Chapter 150 of the Acts of 2024 amended MGL Ch 40A §§1A and 3, and EOHLC's implementing regulation 760 CMR 71.00 ("Protected Use Accessory Dwelling Units") operationalizes the by-right ADU framework. By February 2, 2025 (the regulation's effective date), every standard MA municipality was required to permit Protected Use ADUs by-right in single-family residential zones, with a 1-parking maximum (0 within 0.5 mi of qualifying transit) and no owner-occupancy requirement.

**Boston is structurally different.** Boston operates under a special-act zoning enabling framework, not standard MGL Ch 40A. The exact reach of Ch. 150's preemption framework into Boston is **a known open question** as of 2026-05-03, and BPDA's published guidance frames it as a question requiring Boston-specific zoning amendments rather than automatic flow-through of the state floor.

[source: https://www.bostonplans.org/planning-zoning/zoning-initiatives/neighborhood-housing | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA Neighborhood Housing Zoning page — "Massachusetts' Affordable Homes Act allows units 'under 900 square feet' statewide, but Boston operates under separate Chapter 556 legislation, requiring Boston-specific zoning amendments"]

This file documents what's settled, what's contested, and what the agent should and should not assert in a Boston ADU correction response.

## What's Settled

The state-law-floor terms in MGL Ch 40A §3 (as amended) and 760 CMR 71.00 are:

- **Single ADU per lot** is the Protected Use envelope; additional ADUs require local special permit
- **≤900 sq ft OR ≤50% of primary**, whichever is less
- **No owner-occupancy requirement** — the property owner cannot be required to occupy either dwelling
- **Maximum 1 additional parking space**; **zero within 0.5 mi of commuter rail station, subway station, ferry terminal, or bus station**
- **No special permit may be required** for the first Protected Use ADU on a single-family lot
- **Cities retain authority** over dimensional standards, height, bulk, design (within reasonableness), STR restrictions, historic district review, wetlands review

## What's Contested for Boston

The substantive question: **does the state floor flow through to Boston automatically, or does Boston require a city ordinance amendment to bring each provision into local force?**

Two readings exist in the practitioner community. Both are defensible; neither has been definitively resolved by published EOHLC guidance specific to Boston as of this writing.

### Reading 1: State Law Preempts Boston's Special Act on ADU USE

Under this reading, Ch. 150 amended MGL Ch 40A — Boston's special-act zoning enabling legislation operates within MA's state-law framework and is subject to general-law amendments unless explicitly carved out. The Affordable Homes Act did not carve out Boston. Therefore Boston is bound by the state floor on ADU use, owner-occupancy, parking, and first-ADU special permit rules from February 2, 2025 onward, regardless of whether Boston has updated its zoning code.

Under Reading 1, in any Boston neighborhood — amended or not — the agent can assert state preemption against a corrections demand for owner-occupancy, special permit for first ADU, or parking above the statutory floor.

### Reading 2: Boston's Special Act Requires Local Amendment to Implement Ch. 150

Under this reading, Boston's special-act zoning operates outside the standard MGL Ch 40A framework. Ch. 150's amendments to Ch 40A do not directly bind Boston without a corresponding amendment to Boston's enabling legislation or to the Boston Zoning Code. Until Boston amends each neighborhood Article, Ch. 150's USE preemption is not directly enforceable in Boston, even though the city is committed to implementing equivalent rules through the Neighborhood Housing Zoning initiative. **BPDA's own framing supports Reading 2** in the explicit statement that "Boston operates under separate Chapter 556 legislation, requiring Boston-specific zoning amendments."

Under Reading 2, the agent's preemption argument is weaker in unamended Boston neighborhoods (Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, and all other neighborhoods not yet through PLAN amendment). The contractor may need to either (a) seek a variance through the ZBA, or (b) wait for the neighborhood amendment, or (c) escalate to court for a definitive preemption ruling — an expensive path.

[source: https://www.bostonplans.org/planning-zoning/zoning-initiatives/neighborhood-housing | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA framing of Boston requiring its own zoning amendments]

### What the Agent Should Assert

**Default to a hybrid reading in contractor responses:**

- For **amended neighborhoods (currently Mattapan as of 2026-05-03)**: cite the controlling post-amendment Article + Ch. 150 framework. The state floor and the local article are aligned; preemption analysis is academic. Just cite the local article that implements Ch. 150 protections.
- For **non-amended neighborhoods**: cite Ch. 150 / 760 CMR 71.00 as the state floor, AND cite the practical reality that Boston-specific implementation is in progress per the BPDA initiative. Frame the preemption argument as an option for the contractor to consider with their attorney, not as a definitively-binding rule. Suggest engaging the ZBA in parallel with any preemption argument. Do NOT make a flat preemption assertion as if Boston is a standard Ch 40A municipality.

**Do NOT assert in a Boston response:**

- "Per state law, Boston cannot require X, full stop." The reach of state law into Boston's special-act framework is contested.
- "The corrections letter is preempted and must be withdrawn." This may be true; it may not be. Frame as "we believe this requirement is inconsistent with the state floor; we request the city reconsider in light of Ch. 150 / 760 CMR 71.00 and the BPDA Neighborhood Housing Zoning policy."
- "Boston has been by-right since February 2, 2025." This conflates the state effective date with Boston's actual implementation status.

## The "Bus Station" Question Specific to Boston

The state-law parking exemption applies within 0.5 mi of "commuter rail station, subway station, ferry terminal, or bus station." Boston has the densest MBTA service in the state — Red, Orange, Green, Blue, Mattapan subway lines, three commuter rail terminals (South Station, North Station, Back Bay), multiple ferry terminals, and the Silver Line BRT plus extensive Key Bus Routes.

The agent's default is the conservative reading: "bus station" includes Key Bus Route stops with stations / shelters / printed schedules and the Silver Line BRT stations, but not every flag-pole bus stop. See `transit-parking.md` for the full workflow. EOHLC's specific position on this is pending verification (same 403 issue as above).
