# Boston ADU Permit Process

## Permit Authorities and Their Roles

Boston ADU permitting touches up to five city authorities:

- **Inspectional Services Department (ISD)** — primary authority. Issues building permits, performs plan review, conducts inspections, enforces 780 CMR. Address: 1010 Massachusetts Avenue, 5th Floor. Phone: 617-635-5300. Email: isd@boston.gov. [source: https://www.boston.gov/departments/inspectional-services | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: ISD contact information page]
- **Boston Planning & Development Agency (BPDA)** — zoning authority. Reviews zoning compliance, runs Article 80 design review for projects above thresholds, runs the Neighborhood Housing Zoning initiative driving per-neighborhood ADU amendments. [source: https://www.bostonplans.org | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA agency landing page]
- **Zoning Board of Appeal (ZBA)** — variances and appeals. Triggered when the proposed ADU does not conform to the controlling neighborhood Article (common in pre-amendment neighborhoods).
- **Boston Fire Department (BFD)** — fire-life-safety review. Online portal at https://onlinepermitsandlicenses.boston.gov/bfd/. [source: https://www.boston.gov/boston-permitting | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: Boston permitting gateway page references BFD portal]
- **Boston Landmarks Commission** — historic district review (separate file: `historic-districts.md` forthcoming).
- **Boston Conservation Commission** — wetlands review (filing required within 100 ft of wetland resource area under MGL Ch 131 §40).

## Article 80 Threshold Check (Almost Always: Not Triggered)

Article 80 (Boston Zoning Code) is Boston's project-scale design-review framework. Sub-types:

- **Small Project Review** — projects 20,000 to 50,000 sq ft
- **Large Project Review** — projects exceeding 50,000 sq ft
- **Planned Development Areas (PDA)** — project areas larger than 1 acre
- **Institutional Master Plans** — academic/medical campuses

[source: https://www.bostonplans.org/projects/development-review/what-is-article-80 | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: BPDA Article 80 explainer page]

[source: https://library.municode.com/ma/boston/codes/redevelopment_authority?nodeId=ART80DEREAP | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: Boston Zoning Code Article 80 hosted on Municode]

A single Protected Use ADU is capped at 900 sq ft by state law (760 CMR 71.00). **A 900 sq ft ADU is far below the 20,000 sq ft Small Project Review threshold and does not trigger Article 80 review.** The Article 80 check is therefore a quick pass for almost all single-ADU permits.

When Article 80 is invoked in a Boston ADU correction, treat it as a likely error or an unusual project (multiple ADUs combined with substantial primary-dwelling work, or a campus-adjacent project). Verify the correction's stated square footage against the actual project scope before responding.

## ISD Submittal — Technical Specifications

Verified ISD plan submission requirements:

- Plans must be **stamped and signed by a registered design professional on every sheet** [source: https://www.boston.gov/departments/inspectional-services/submitting-building-plans-online | retrieved: 2026-05-03 | citation: ISD electronic submission guidelines]
- File format: **CAD converted directly to PDF** (DWG to PDF conversion path) [source: ibid.]
- **Maximum file size: 75 MB** [source: ibid.]
- **AIA page naming convention** required [source: ibid. — page references "American Institute of Architect's page naming convention"]
- Plans must be legible with accurate scale on each sheet [source: ibid.]

For non-electronic or supplemental questions: isdpzreview@boston.gov for upload issues; isd@boston.gov general; 617-635-5300 phone.

## Plan Sheets — Generic List, Boston-Specific Detail TBD

A typical Boston ADU permit submission includes the following plan sheets (generic MA-residential list; Boston-specific checklist requires direct ISD verification — TBD):

- Cover sheet with code summary, project data, and AIA-convention page index
- Site plan (parcel boundaries, existing structures, proposed ADU location, setbacks dimensioned, utility connections, drainage)
- Floor plans (existing and proposed; dimensioned; room-use labels; egress windows located)
- Building elevations (all sides)
- Building sections (typical wall, roof, foundation; insulation values noted)
- Structural plans and calculations (stamped by structural engineer for new construction)
- Energy code compliance documentation (HERS rating per Specialized Opt-In Code; envelope, mechanical, lighting compliance)
- Mechanical, plumbing, electrical (MPE) plans — typically separate sheets
- Fire separation and rated-assembly details (between ADU and primary; per IRC R302)
- Egress demonstration (window dimensions, escape path)
- Smoke and CO alarm plan (per MGL Ch 148 §26F / §26F½)
- Owner authorization (if applicant is not owner)
- Contractor license verification
- Workers compensation certification

## Review Timeline (Approximate)

- **Administrative by-right ADU permit:** 2–6 weeks if the submittal is complete and the parcel is in an amended neighborhood (Mattapan; future Roslindale/West Roxbury/Hyde Park) or otherwise by-right under preemption analysis
- **+ Landmarks Commission review:** add 4–8 weeks
- **+ Conservation Commission review (within 100 ft of wetland resource area):** add 8–12 weeks
- **+ ZBA variance (typically required in pre-amendment neighborhoods for a non-conforming ADU):** add 6–10 weeks for hearing and decision

## Workflow for the Agent

When a Boston ADU correction touches the permit process:

1. **Identify which authority issued the correction.** Different language for an ISD code-compliance issue vs. a BPDA zoning issue vs. a Landmarks comment.
2. **Quick Article 80 check.** If the correction invokes Article 80 against a single ADU under 900 sq ft, the threshold is almost certainly not met — flag the correction as likely-erroneous and respond accordingly.
3. **Check submittal completeness against the generic plan-sheet list above.** If the correction is "missing X sheet," verify whether X is on the project's submission package.
4. **Quote ISD-published technical specs (stamped/signed, AIA naming, 75MB max) directly when responding to administrative submittal corrections.**
5. **For inspection or fee corrections, do not assert specific Boston values without re-verifying** against the current ISD page. Instead, cite the state-skill-level rule (780 CMR administrative provisions) and request the city clarify the specific Boston cite.
