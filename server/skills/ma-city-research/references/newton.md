## Zoning Framework

Newton has a traditional single-family-district-heavy zoning ordinance:
- **Single Residence 1 (SR1)** — largest lot / most restrictive
- **Single Residence 2 (SR2)**
- **Single Residence 3 (SR3)** — smaller lots
- **Multi Residence 1/2/3** — multi-family
- Various business and mixed-use districts

The ADU by-right provisions under Ch 150 of 2024 apply in all the SR districts.

## ADU Provisions

Newton has updated its Zoning Ordinance post-Ch 150 to include ADU provisions consistent with state law. Verify exact section numbers via Municode.

Post-Ch 150 compliance:
- ADUs allowed by-right in SR1/SR2/SR3 districts consistent with state law
- Size per state: 900 sq ft or 50% of primary (lesser)
- No owner-occupancy requirement
- Parking per state (Newton has MBTA commuter rail stops — Newton Highlands, Newton Centre, Auburndale, Newtonville — so many lots qualify for the 0.5 mi transit exemption)

## Dimensional Standards (Typical)

Newton's dimensional standards vary significantly by district. Approximate values:

**Single Residence 1 (SR1):**
- Min lot area: 10,000–15,000 sq ft typical
- Front setback: 25–30 ft
- Side setback: 10–15 ft each
- Rear setback: 25–30 ft
- Max height: 36 ft typical
- Lot coverage: typically 25%

**Single Residence 2 (SR2):**
- Min lot area: 7,000–10,000 sq ft typical
- Side setback: 7.5–10 ft each
- Max height: 36 ft typical
- Lot coverage: typically 30–35%

**Single Residence 3 (SR3):**
- Min lot area: 5,000–7,000 sq ft typical
- Side setback: 7.5 ft each
- Max height: 36 ft typical
- Lot coverage: typically 30–40%

**Accessory structure setbacks** typically permit reduced distances vs. primary structure. Verify per-district.

**Verification note:** Values are illustrative. Always cite from the specific Municode text before use.

## Design Standards

Newton applies reasonable design standards. Common expectations:
- Roofline and materials consistent with neighborhood
- Scale relative to primary structure
- Window placement considering privacy to neighboring lots

No discretionary design board review for typical by-right ADUs.

## Historic Districts

Newton has several locally-designated historic districts:
- Chestnut Hill Historic District
- Upper Falls Historic District
- Newton Centre Historic District
- And others (verify current list via Newton Historical Commission)

Exterior modifications in these districts require Historical Commission review.

## Stretch / Specialized Energy Code

Newton has adopted the **Specialized Opt-In Code**. Same implications as Cambridge/Boston:
- All-electric heating (heat pumps) or renewable offset
- Higher envelope R-values
- Solar-ready provisions

## Review Timeline

- Administrative by-right ADU: 3–6 weeks typical
- With historic review: add 4–6 weeks

## Known Gotchas for Newton ADU Permits

1. **Larger lots, larger setbacks** — Newton's SR1 especially has generous setbacks; ADU placement requires careful site planning
2. **Sloped lots common** — Newton's topography creates height-limit edge cases
3. **Mature trees / street trees** — tree preservation ordinance may affect siting
4. **Historic districts** — several neighborhoods fall within district boundaries
5. **Specialized Energy Code** — full compliance including HERS rating and envelope performance

## Preemption Watch Items

Newton's updated bylaw generally aligns with Ch 150. Watch for:
- Legacy references to owner-occupancy in pre-Ch 150 bylaw sections
- Historic district overlays conflating zoning preemption with MGL Ch 40C separate authority (don't confuse)
