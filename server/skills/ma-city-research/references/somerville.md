## Zoning Framework

Somerville rewrote its zoning ordinance comprehensively in 2019 ("Somerville by Design" / "Form-Based Code" influence). Districts are organized differently from most MA cities:

- **Neighborhood Residence (NR)** — primarily single-family/small multi-family
- **Residence A (RA)** — older district designation still present in some areas
- **Mid-Rise / Urban Residence** — denser zones
- Special districts (Assembly Square, Union Square, etc.)

Somerville was an ADU early adopter. The 2019 rewrite included provisions for ADUs, and 2022 amendments made the process simpler.

## ADU Provisions

Somerville's ADU provisions are in the zoning ordinance (consult current section numbers via eCode360). Post-Ch 150 compliance:
- ADUs allowed by-right in single-family districts consistent with state law
- Size per state
- No owner-occupancy requirement
- Parking per state (Somerville is extensively transit-served — Red Line, Green Line extension, commuter rail at Porter)

**Important:** In Neighborhood Residence zones, the 2019 code limits total units to 3 per lot, one of which must be permanently affordable if the lot reaches 3 units. This can affect ADU feasibility if the primary structure is already a duplex.

## Dimensional Standards

Somerville's form-based approach regulates differently than typical MA. Key elements:
- Front yard setback
- Side yard setback
- Rear yard setback
- Building height in stories + feet
- Usable open space
- Lot coverage

Verify specific NR zone dimensional numbers via the eCode360 viewer; values have been updated multiple times.

## Design Standards

Somerville has form-based design standards addressing:

For ADUs, the standards typically track the primary structure's style. Reasonable compliance is usually straightforward.

## Review Timeline

- With historic: add 4-6 weeks

## Known Gotchas for Somerville ADU Permits

1. **NR zone 3-unit limit with affordability trigger** — if primary is a 2-family in NR, adding ADU makes 3 units and may trigger affordability requirement. Check before designing.
2. **Form-based code compliance** — design standards more explicit than most MA cities; plan accordingly
3. **Specialized Energy Code** — full compliance required
4. **Density** — Somerville is denser than most MA cities; lot coverage commonly a binding constraint
5. **Parking** — almost everything in Somerville qualifies for the 0.5 mi transit exemption given new Green Line extension

## Preemption Watch Items

Somerville's 2019/2022 updates generally align with state-friendly ADU policy. Preemption conflicts rare. Main watch item:
- The 3-unit limit in NR zones with affordability trigger — arguable if it conflicts with state floor for single ADU in certain configurations. Case-by-case.

## Historical Note

Somerville pioneered ADU-friendly policy in MA. 2019 zoning rewrite allowed backyard cottages. 2022 amendments simplified the process. This context is useful when working with Somerville officials who tend to be supportive of ADU projects.
