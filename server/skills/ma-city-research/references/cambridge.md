## Zoning Framework

Cambridge has a unified Zoning Ordinance codified on Municode. Residential districts include:
- **Residence A-1** — single-family, most restrictive
- **Residence A-2** — single-family, less restrictive
- **Residence B** — two-family / small multi-family
- **Residence C-1 / C-2 / C-3** — multi-family, varying density
- Plus special districts (Harvard Square, Central Square, etc.)

ADU provisions apply across residential districts where single-family use is permitted.

## ADU Provisions

Cambridge was an early ADU-friendly municipality. The zoning ordinance explicitly permits accessory apartments/ADUs with dimensional standards reflecting the city's density.

Post-Ch 150 compliance:
- ADUs allowed by-right in single-family districts consistent with state law
- 900 sq ft / 50% cap per state
- No owner-occupancy requirement (Cambridge pre-2025 also did not require owner-occupancy in most cases)
- Parking per state (Cambridge has MBTA Red Line, extensive bus service, so most lots qualify for the 0.5 mi transit exemption)

## Dimensional Standards

**Residence A-1:**
- Min lot area: 6,000 sq ft typical
- Front setback: 20 ft
- Side setback: 10 ft each / 20 ft total
- Rear setback: 25 ft
- Max height: 35 ft
- Max FAR: 0.5

**Residence A-2:**
- Min lot area: 5,000 sq ft typical
- Front setback: 15 ft
- Side setback: 7.5 ft each / 15 ft total
- Rear setback: 20 ft
- Max height: 35 ft
- Max FAR: 0.75

**Residence B:**
- Min lot area: 5,000 sq ft
- Side setback: 7.5 ft each / 15 ft total
- Max FAR: 0.75

**Accessory structure setbacks** typically allow reduced side and rear setbacks compared to primary structure, but verify per district.

**Verification note:** Numbers above are illustrative based on typical Cambridge dimensional standards. Verify specific district bylaw text at the zoning ordinance URL before citing.

## Design Standards

Cambridge applies generally "reasonable regulations." Design review typically involves:
- Materials consistent with neighborhood
- Window placement not unduly visible from public way in some contexts
- Scale relative to primary structure

No discretionary design board review for typical by-right ADUs.

## Historic Districts

Cambridge has several locally-designated historic districts:
- Old Cambridge
- Harvard Square
- Avon Hill
- Mid-Cambridge
- Half-Crown/Marsh

Within these, Cambridge Historical Commission review applies for exterior changes. Process: 4-6 weeks typical.

Additionally, Cambridge has a Neighborhood Conservation District (NCD) program covering areas like Half Crown-Marsh NCD, Avon Hill NCD — similar review requirements.

## Stretch / Specialized Energy Code

Cambridge has adopted the **Specialized Opt-In Code**. Same implications as Boston:
- All-electric heating or renewable offset
- Higher envelope performance
- Solar-ready provisions

## Short-Term Rental

Cambridge requires STR registration and has restrictions on non-owner-occupied STRs. Verify current rules before designing for STR use.

## Submittal Requirements

- Building permit application
- Site plan with dimensional compliance demonstration
- Floor plans, elevations, sections
- Structural calcs if required
- Specialized Code energy compliance documentation
- Mechanical/plumbing/electrical coordination
- Historic review if applicable

## Fees

Approximate (verify current):
- Building permit fee: based on construction valuation, typically 1-2% of valuation
- Plumbing, mechanical, electrical: separate, ~$200-400 each
- Historic review: modest flat fee if applicable

## Review Timeline

- Administrative building permit for by-right ADU: 3-6 weeks typical
- With historic review: add 4-6 weeks
- With conservation review: add 6-10 weeks

## Known Gotchas for Cambridge ADU Permits

1. **Tight lots** — Cambridge has many small lots; dimensional compliance for detached ADUs can be tough. Conversion is common.
2. **NCD overlay** — Many Cambridge neighborhoods have NCD designation beyond formal historic districts; check Cambridge Historical Commission GIS.
3. **Existing non-conforming structures** — primary dwelling may be non-conforming; interactions with ADU addition matter.
4. **Parking** — most of Cambridge is within 0.5 mi of Red Line or major bus; zero parking required.
5. **Specialized Energy Code** — full compliance is a significant cost and design factor; account for it early.

## Preemption Watch Items

Cambridge was relatively ADU-friendly pre-Ch 150, so preemption conflicts are less common than in more restrictive cities. Watch for:
- Legacy requirements in older bylaw text not yet updated
- Site plan review processes that drift into discretionary territory
