# Elevations & Sections Checklist — Massachusetts ADU

This file defines what must appear on the elevation, roof plan, and building section sheets. Elevations prove height compliance and show envelope details; sections prove wall and roof assemblies meet code; both are where the Specialized Opt-In Energy Code envelope is documented.

## Scope

Applies to all four exterior elevations (typically front, rear, left, right) plus typical wall and roof sections. For attached ADUs, sections must show the dwelling-unit fire separation assembly. ## Required Elements

### 1. Building Height Documentation

**Must include:**
- Overall height dimension from existing grade to highest point of roof
- Height measurement methodology (varies by municipality — eaves, ridge, average grade, specific reference point)
- Compliance comparison: required (per zoning) vs. provided
- Height shown on at least two elevations for verification

**Code basis:**
- Municipal zoning bylaw — height limit reserved to localities under MGL Ch 40A §3 [source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40A/Section3 | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: MGL Ch 40A §3 dimensional regulation reserved]
- 780 CMR — references zoning compliance

**Visual identification:** Height dimension lines on elevation; compliance table.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Height measured to ridge when bylaw uses average grade-to-eaves
- Height shown on only one elevation when multiple are required
- Sloped lot with grade variation not addressed in measurement

**Confidence:** HIGH for presence; MEDIUM for measurement methodology accuracy.

### 2. Exterior Wall Construction

**Must include:**
- Wall section reference key on each elevation
- Cladding material (siding, brick, stucco, etc.)
- Trim and detail callouts
- Window and door rough opening locations matching floor plans

**Code basis:**
- 780 CMR / IRC Chapter 7 — exterior wall covering [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\massachusetts-adu\references\780-cmr-essentials.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: state skill 780 CMR exterior wall reference]
- IRC R302.1 — fire separation distance affects exterior wall rating

**Visual identification:** Wall material hatching on elevation; section keys at corners.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Window head/sill/jamb details not provided
- Cladding material not called out
- Trim details inconsistent with section

**Confidence:** HIGH for material identification.

### 3. Exterior Wall Fire-Resistance Rating (When Triggered)

**Must include (when ADU is within 5 ft of property line):**
- Fire-resistance rating of exterior wall (1-hour typical when <3 ft to lot line)
- Opening protection (limited openings within 3-5 ft of lot line)
- Eave projection limit (eaves within 2 ft of lot line need additional protection)

**Code basis:**
- IRC R302.1 and Table R302.1(1) — exterior wall fire-resistance based on fire separation distance [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\massachusetts-adu\references\780-cmr-essentials.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: state skill IRC R302.1 reference]
- Boston-specific note: dense urban lots routinely trigger this rule [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\boston-adu\references\building-codes.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: Boston building-codes.md fire separation note]

**Visual identification:** Fire-resistance callout on wall section; projection limits noted.

**Common deficiencies:**
- ADU shown 2 ft from lot line without exterior wall rating callout
- Window in rated wall without opening protection
- Eave projection exceeding limit without rating

**Confidence:** HIGH for distance-to-lot-line presence; MEDIUM for assembly-spec correctness.

### 4. Roof Plan

**Must include:**
- Roof slope direction (typically arrows showing flow)
- Roof material (shingle type, metal, membrane)
- Drainage termination (gutters, downspouts, scuppers)
- Penetrations (vents, skylights, solar PV mounting if applicable)

**Code basis:**
- 780 CMR / IRC Chapter 9 — roof assemblies and roof drainage [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\massachusetts-adu\references\780-cmr-essentials.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: state skill IRC roof reference]

**Visual identification:** Top-down view of roof on dedicated plan or elevation inset.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Roof slope not called out
- Drainage path unclear
- Solar PV mounting shown without structural callout

**Confidence:** HIGH.

### 5. Solar-Ready Provisions (Specialized Opt-In Code Municipalities)

**Must include (for projects in Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline, etc.):**
- Note that solar PV conduit pathway is provided
- Roof orientation and shading documented (south-facing primary roof preferred)
- Structural roof load capacity for future solar PV array
- Electrical panel capacity for solar interconnection

**Code basis:**
- 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC (Specialized Opt-In Code) — solar-ready provisions [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\boston-adu\references\energy-stretch-code.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: Boston energy-stretch-code.md solar-ready provisions]

**Visual identification:** Note on roof plan or elevation; structural detail referenced.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Solar-ready provisions omitted entirely (frequent Boston ADU correction)
- Solar PV claimed for renewable offset pathway but no structural detail

**Confidence:** HIGH for Specialized Opt-In municipalities; not applicable elsewhere.

### 6. Building Sections

**Must include:**
- At least one full building section through the ADU
- Wall, floor, and roof assembly callouts with R-values
- Floor-to-ceiling heights at each level
- Foundation type and depth
- Window head and sill heights at egress locations

**Code basis:**
- 780 CMR 107.3.1 — construction documents must show construction details
- IRC Chapter 11 / 225 CMR 22 — energy code envelope requirements [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\boston-adu\references\energy-stretch-code.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: Boston energy-stretch-code.md envelope R-values]

**Visual identification:** Vertical cut through structure showing layered assemblies.

**Common deficiencies:**
- R-values not called out (or below code minimum for the climate zone)
- Floor-to-ceiling height below 7 ft (IRC R305 minimum)
- Section doesn't extend through critical conditions (e.g., the converted basement bedroom)

**Confidence:** HIGH for presence; MEDIUM for assembly correctness.

### 7. Energy Code Envelope (Specialized Opt-In Detail)

**Must include (in Specialized Opt-In municipalities):**
- Wall R-value (typically R-21 cavity + R-5 continuous, ≈R-26 effective)
- Roof/ceiling R-value (R-49 to R-60)
- Floor/slab edge R-value (R-19 cavity + R-10 perimeter)
- Window U-factor (≤0.27)
- Air leakage target (≤3.0 ACH50 tested)

**Code basis:**
- 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC (Specialized Opt-In Code) [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\boston-adu\references\energy-stretch-code.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: Boston energy-stretch-code.md envelope targets]

**Visual identification:** Section assembly callouts with R-values; energy compliance note block.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Wall R-value cited at R-19 (Base Code) when Specialized requires R-21 + R-5
- Window U-factor at 0.30 (Base) when Specialized requires 0.27
- Air leakage target at 5.0 ACH50 (Base) when Specialized requires 3.0
- Missing entirely in Specialized municipalities

**Confidence:** HIGH for Specialized municipalities once verifier knows the locality.

### 8. Window and Door Schedule (Coordinated with Floor Plan)

**Must include:**
- Window type, dimensions, U-factor, SHGC for each window
- Door type, dimensions, fire rating (if applicable)
- Egress window callouts matching floor plan

**Code basis:**
- 780 CMR / IRC Chapter 6 — windows and doors
- IRC R310 — egress dimensions [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\massachusetts-adu\references\780-cmr-essentials.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: state skill IRC R310 egress reference]

**Visual identification:** Schedule block typically on elevation sheet or A-series.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Window schedule lists U-factor inconsistent with section R-value targets
- Egress window listed without 5.7 sq ft net clear demonstration
- Door fire rating omitted at dwelling-unit separation

**Confidence:** HIGH for schedule presence; MEDIUM for compliance correctness.

### 9. Historic District Design Compliance (When Applicable)

**Must include (only if parcel is in a Boston Landmarks district or other locally-designated historic district):**
- Materials and detailing consistent with district guidelines
- Window proportions and trim profiles compatible with neighborhood character
- Reference to Landmarks Commission Certificate of Appropriateness or determination

**Code basis:**
- MGL Ch 40C — Historic Districts Act [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\boston-adu\references\historic-districts.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: MGL Ch 40C governs historic districts]
- Specific Boston ordinance per district [source: c:\dev\permitmonkey\server\skills\boston-adu\references\historic-districts.md | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: Boston historic-districts.md district list]

**Visual identification:** Notes on elevations; trim profiles consistent with historic precedent.

**Common deficiencies:**
- Modern detailing on Beacon Hill / Back Bay / South End parcel (will be flagged by Landmarks regardless of zoning compliance)
- Vinyl windows where district guidelines require wood
- Asphalt shingle where district requires slate

**Confidence:** HIGH for triggering district presence; LOW for stylistic compatibility (subjective; require Landmarks review).

## Quick-Review Decision Tree

```
Are all four exterior elevations provided?
├── NO → Deficient. Request missing elevations.
└── YES → Continue.

Is overall building height dimensioned and compared to zoning limit?
├── NO → Deficient. Request height compliance.
└── YES → Continue.

Is the ADU within 5 ft of any property line?
├── NO → Skip exterior wall fire rating check.
└── YES → Verify IRC R302.1 fire-resistance rating callout.

For Specialized Opt-In municipality (Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline, et al.):
Are envelope R-values, U-factors, and ACH50 target called out?
├── NO → Deficient. Cite 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC.
└── YES → Continue.

Does the building section show wall/floor/roof assemblies with R-values?
├── NO → Deficient. Request typical wall section.
└── YES → Continue.

If parcel is in a historic district, are detailing notes consistent with district guidelines?
├── NO → Flag for Landmarks Commission review.
└── YES → Elevations & sections pass.
```

## Reviewer Confidence Notes

- **HIGH confidence:** Number of elevations, height dimension presence, R-value callouts, lot-line distance.
- **MEDIUM confidence:** Height measurement methodology, exterior wall fire rating assembly correctness, section continuity through critical conditions.
- **LOW confidence:** Stylistic compatibility with historic district (require Landmarks review), aesthetic/character judgments (out of scope per Gov. Code analog — see SKILL.md note on objective standards).

## When to Escalate to Human Review

- Lot-line distance ≤3 ft with limited openings (assembly correctness review)
- Specialized Opt-In envelope mismatch (engineer-stamped energy modeling needed)
- Historic district parcel without Landmarks certificate
- Roof structure for solar PV mounting (engineer review)
