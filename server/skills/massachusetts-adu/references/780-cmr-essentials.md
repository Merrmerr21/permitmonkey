# 780 CMR — MA State Building Code (Essentials for ADUs)

**Code:** 780 CMR — Massachusetts State Building Code
**Edition:** 10th Edition (effective Oct 2023, adopts IRC 2021 / IBC 2021 with MA amendments)
**Authority:** Board of Building Regulations and Standards (BBRS)
**Source:** https://www.mass.gov/state-building-code

## Scope For ADU Analysis

780 CMR governs the physical construction of the ADU regardless of zoning. The ADU is almost always either a new one- or two-family dwelling structure (780 CMR Ch 51, which adopts IRC 2021) OR an alteration to an existing dwelling. Which path applies determines which chapter the corrections item invokes.

## Classification of ADUs Under 780 CMR

- **Detached new-construction ADU** — a separate small dwelling unit. Typically classified under IRC (780 CMR Ch 51) as a one-family dwelling on the same lot as another one-family dwelling.
- **Attached new-construction ADU** — addition to the primary dwelling. IRC applies to the new construction; existing Building Code applies to alterations of the primary.
- **Conversion ADU** (garage conversion, basement finish, attic conversion) — alteration of an existing structure. Applies the MA Existing Building Code provisions plus IRC for new elements.

## Commonly Cited Sections in ADU Corrections

### Egress (IRC R310 adopted by 780 CMR)

Every sleeping room requires an emergency escape and rescue opening. For ADUs, this means every bedroom. Common correction: "Provide compliant emergency egress from Bedroom 1 per R310." Response: specify the window or door, its dimensions (min 5.7 sq ft net clear, min 24" height, min 20" width, max sill height 44" AFF), and location on plans.

### Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Alarms (IRC R314, R315; MA amendments)

MA has specific state amendments on alarm requirements that exceed IRC baseline. Required:
- Smoke alarms in every sleeping room, outside each sleeping area, on every story
- Interconnected if ADU is new construction
- CO alarms within 10 ft of sleeping areas on every story if ADU has fuel-burning appliances or attached garage
- Hardwired with battery backup in new construction; battery-only may be acceptable in existing conditions

MA also requires compliance with MGL Ch 148 §26F (smoke detectors) and §26F½ (CO detectors).

### Fire Separation (IRC R302)

**Attached ADUs** require fire-resistance-rated separation between the ADU and the primary dwelling. Typically 1-hour fire-rated assembly (R302.3) — wall and floor/ceiling if stacked.

**Detached ADUs** have fire-separation requirements based on distance from the primary dwelling and lot lines (R302.1, Table R302.1(1)). Common triggers:
- <3 ft from lot line: exterior wall fire-rated 1 hr (structural frame, exterior walls, floors)
- 3-5 ft from lot line: openings limited, protection required
- ≥5 ft from lot line: standard construction

### Energy Code (IRC Ch 11 as modified by MA Stretch / Specialized Code)

ADUs are subject to whichever energy code the municipality has adopted:
- **Base Code** — IECC 2021 with MA amendments (roughly 30% of MA municipalities)
- **Stretch Energy Code** — higher performance (most MA municipalities)
- **Specialized Opt-In Code** — requires electrification or renewable offset (~60 municipalities including Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline)

Contractors frequently get corrections on HERS rating, envelope insulation values, mechanical ventilation, or renewable energy offsets under the Specialized Code.

### Mechanical Ventilation (IRC M1505, IMC 403 as applied)

ADUs under 1,200 sq ft still require whole-house mechanical ventilation per IRC M1505. Common correction: "Provide continuous mechanical ventilation compliant with ASHRAE 62.2 referenced in IRC M1505."

### Plumbing (IRC Ch 25-33 with MA 248 CMR amendments — NOTE: plumbing code is separate)

Plumbing is governed by 248 CMR (Massachusetts Plumbing Code), not 780 CMR directly. ADUs need a compliant drain, waste, vent (DWV) system, water supply, and fixture schedules per 248 CMR. Cross-reference with city plumbing inspector.

### Electrical (780 CMR adopts NEC 2023 with MA amendments via 527 CMR 12)

ADUs need separate electrical service OR a dedicated sub-panel from the primary. Corrections often invoke:
- Load calculations
- Grounding/bonding
- AFCI/GFCI protection per NEC 210
- MA amendments in 527 CMR 12

## One-Time vs. Ongoing Code

If the corrections letter cites an old edition (e.g., 8th or 9th Edition), note:
- 10th Edition is current as of Oct 2023
- Projects permitted before that date may be grandfathered into the prior edition
- Projects permitted after must comply with 10th

## Compliance Paths

780 CMR generally allows two compliance paths:
1. **Prescriptive** — meet the specific requirements as written
2. **Performance** — demonstrate equivalent performance (engineer-sealed)

ADU corrections most commonly arise on prescriptive path. If the contractor wants to argue performance equivalence, that's a professional engineer or registered architect deliverable.

## Gaps in This Reference

This file covers high-frequency ADU corrections topics. Deeper topics not covered here:
- Structural design (load paths, seismic, wind) — governed by IRC Ch 3-8 and IBC where applicable
- Foundations — frost depth (MA 48" typical), bearing capacity
- Decks (IRC R507 with MA amendments)
- Solar PV integration

When a correction invokes one of these gaps, do an on-demand web_fetch of the specific 780 CMR section and cite it inline. Do not guess.
