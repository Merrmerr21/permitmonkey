# 248 CMR — MA Uniform State Plumbing Code (Essentials for ADUs)

**Code:** 248 CMR — Massachusetts Uniform State Plumbing and Gas Code
**Authority:** Board of State Examiners of Plumbers and Gas Fitters
**Source:** https://www.mass.gov/regulations/248-CMR [source: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/248-CMR | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: 248 CMR master regulation]

## Scope For ADU Analysis

248 CMR governs the plumbing scope of any ADU permit independent of 780 CMR (the building code). Plumbing for the ADU requires a separate plumbing permit and inspection by the local plumbing inspector.

The agent's job in plan review: confirm the plumbing schematic shows the right fixtures, correct DWV (drain-waste-vent) connections, and proper water-supply sizing. Then flag missing items for the plumbing permit (which is filed separately from the building permit but reviewed in coordination).

## 248 CMR 10 — Uniform State Plumbing Code

### Required Plumbing Elements For An ADU

Every ADU must, at minimum, contain a kitchen sink, a bathroom (toilet + lavatory + tub or shower), and water heating. The plumbing schematic on the plans must show:

- DWV layout for every fixture (sink, lavatory, toilet, tub/shower)
- Vent terminations (typically through-roof, sized per 248 CMR 10)
- Trap arms and trap weirs
- Water-supply lines (cold and hot) sized to the fixtures
- Shut-off valves at each fixture and at the ADU service entry
- Water heater size and recovery rate (electric or fossil-fuel)

[source: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/248-CMR | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: 248 CMR 10 plumbing fixture requirements]

### Connection To Existing Service (Conversion / Attached ADU)

When an ADU is added to an existing dwelling, the new plumbing typically taps the existing service. The agent must verify:

- Existing service capacity is adequate for the additional fixture units
- The existing meter is sized for combined demand (or a separate meter is added)
- DWV connection to the building drain has adequate slope and capacity
- Shut-off valve installed at the connection point so the ADU can be isolated

For detached ADUs, a new water service from the street main may be required. This is a MWRA / municipal water department coordination — start early; permits can take 6-12 weeks.

### Common Corrections

- Vent termination not shown (must terminate through roof or via approved AAV per 248 CMR 10)
- Water heater not sized on plans
- Hot-water expansion tank omitted (required where backflow prevention exists at service)
- Shut-off valve missing at ADU service entry
- Backflow prevention not shown on service entrance to ADU

## 248 CMR 4-7 — Gas Fitting

If the ADU will have any gas appliance (range, water heater, dryer, fireplace), the gas piping requires a gas fitting permit and inspection. [source: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/248-CMR | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: 248 CMR gas fitting provisions]

### When Gas Fitting Triggers

- New gas service to a detached ADU: full new service from utility (Eversource, National Grid, Berkshire Gas) plus piping.
- Extended gas service to an attached ADU: tap from existing service, run new piping to fixture.
- Conversion ADU repurposing existing gas-fired appliance: typically just the connection / shut-off needs review.

### Specialized Opt-In Code Interaction

Municipalities adopting 225 CMR 22 Specialized Opt-In Code (Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, Newton, Brookline, et al.) push the all-electric pathway as the default. For new ADUs in those cities, gas fitting may simply not apply — verify the fuel source decision before assuming gas piping is needed. [source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/specialized-stretch-code | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: 225 CMR 22 Appendix RC all-electric pathway]

### Common Gas Corrections

- Gas line not pressure-tested before drywall close-in (required by the gas fitter inspector)
- Combustion air for atmospheric water heater not provided (when applicable)
- CO alarm omitted in dwelling unit with new gas appliance (also a MGL Ch 148 §26F½ issue)

## Heat Pump Water Heater Considerations

A common Specialized Opt-In Code outcome for ADUs is a heat pump water heater (HPWH). Plumbing implications:

- Condensate drain required from the HPWH (gravity to floor drain or condensate pump)
- Air clearance / volume around the unit (manufacturer-specified)
- Electrical: 240 V dedicated circuit (NEC / 527 CMR 12)

This crosses 248 CMR (plumbing), 527 CMR 12 (electrical), and 225 CMR 22 (energy code). The agent should expect coordination corrections at the intersection.

## Permit Workflow

For an ADU project, a typical permit sequence is:

1. Building permit (780 CMR) — the umbrella permit
2. Plumbing permit (248 CMR 10) — filed by the licensed plumber, independent of the building permit
3. Gas fitting permit (248 CMR 4-7) — filed by the gas fitter, independent
4. Electrical permit (527 CMR 12) — filed by the electrician, independent

All four permits must be in place AND inspections passed before the ADU receives a Certificate of Occupancy.
