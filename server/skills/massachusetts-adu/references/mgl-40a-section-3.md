# MGL Chapter 40A § 3 — Uses and Structures Permitted

**Chapter:** 40A — The Zoning Act
**Section:** 3
**Post-amendment status:** Amended by St. 2024, c. 150, § 8, effective Feb 2, 2025
**Source:** https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleVII/Chapter40A/Section3

## The ADU-specific paragraphs (post-amendment)

### Paragraph 1 — The By-Right Protection

> "No zoning ordinance or by-law shall prohibit, unreasonably restrict or require a special permit or other discretionary zoning approval for the use of land or structures for a single accessory dwelling unit, or the rental thereof, in a single-family residential zoning district; provided, that the use may be subject to reasonable regulations, including site plan review, regulations concerning dimensional setbacks and the bulk and height of structures and may be subject to restrictions and prohibitions on short-term rental."

### Paragraph 2 — Occupancy and Parking

> "The use of land or structures for an accessory dwelling unit under this paragraph shall not require owner occupancy of either the accessory dwelling unit or the principal dwelling; provided, that not more than 1 additional parking space shall be required for an accessory dwelling unit; and provided further, that no additional parking space shall be required for an accessory dwelling located not more than 0.5 miles from a commuter rail station, subway station, ferry terminal or bus station."

## The "Unreasonable Restriction" Question

The statute prohibits "unreasonable" restriction, which is not self-defining. Early EOHLC guidance (via 760 CMR 71.00) clarifies some bounds:

- Dimensional setbacks that effectively make no ADU buildable on typical lots = unreasonable
- Design review that materially delays or blocks the by-right use = unreasonable
- Fees that substantially exceed cost recovery = potentially unreasonable

**Practical application for PermitMonkey:** When a contractor gets a correction that invokes a local bylaw we think is unreasonable, the response letter should:
1. Acknowledge the local bylaw
2. Cite MGL Ch 40A § 3's "unreasonable" standard
3. Offer a reasonable-compliance alternative if one exists
4. Only invoke preemption as a fallback if the local bylaw is flatly inconsistent with the state floor

## Non-ADU Paragraphs in §3

Section 3 covers many other preempted uses (agriculture, religious, educational, child care, wireless, solar, family day care). Those paragraphs are NOT relevant to ADU analysis and should not be confused with the ADU paragraphs. ADU analysis starts and ends with the two paragraphs above (plus the §1A definition).

## Historical Context

Before St. 2024, c. 150, ADU regulation was entirely local. ~125 MA municipalities had ADU provisions of varying restrictiveness; most required special permits or prohibited them entirely. The amendment replaces this patchwork with a statewide floor.
