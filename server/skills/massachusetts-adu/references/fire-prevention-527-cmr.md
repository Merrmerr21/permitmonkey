# 527 CMR — MA Fire Prevention (Essentials for ADUs)

**Code:** 527 CMR — Massachusetts Comprehensive Fire Safety Code
**Authority:** Department of Fire Services (DFS), Board of Fire Prevention Regulations (BFPR)
**Source:** https://www.mass.gov/orgs/department-of-fire-services [source: https://www.mass.gov/orgs/department-of-fire-services | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: DFS authority over 527 CMR]

## Scope For ADU Analysis

527 CMR governs fire-safety provisions for ADUs that are NOT addressed by 780 CMR (the building code). The key intersections for an ADU permit:

- **527 CMR 1** — Comprehensive Fire Safety Code (life-safety provisions, NFPA 1 base with MA amendments)
- **527 CMR 12** — Massachusetts Electrical Code (NEC 2023 base with MA amendments)
- **MGL Ch 148 §§ 26F, 26F½** — Smoke alarm and CO alarm Statewide Standards (statutory, enforced by DFS and local fire chief)

[source: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/527-CMR-12-massachusetts-electrical-code | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: 527 CMR 12 Massachusetts Electrical Code]

## 527 CMR 12 — Electrical Code (NEC 2023 + MA Amendments)

### When 527 CMR 12 Triggers In An ADU Permit

ANY new wiring (new circuits, new panel, sub-panel for ADU, dedicated branches for heat pump or HVAC, kitchen fixture wiring) requires permit and inspection by the local wiring inspector. This is in addition to the building permit.

### Common ADU Electrical Provisions

- **AFCI protection** — required on most 120 V branch circuits in dwelling units (NEC 210.12 as adopted by 527 CMR 12). Living areas, bedrooms, kitchens.
- **GFCI protection** — required on receptacles serving kitchens, bathrooms, laundry, exterior, garages, basements (NEC 210.8 as adopted).
- **Receptacle spacing** — wall outlets every 12 ft along living-area walls (NEC 210.52). Counter receptacles every 24 in.
- **Service capacity** — primary panel must have headroom for ADU sub-panel; otherwise service upgrade required. Common reason for utility-coordination delay.

### Common ADU Electrical Corrections

- AFCI not provided on bedroom circuits
- Tamper-resistant receptacles missing in living areas
- Insufficient existing service capacity for ADU sub-panel without service upgrade analysis
- Smoke / CO alarm wiring not interconnected (when interconnection required for new construction)

## MGL Ch 148 §§ 26F and 26F½ — Smoke and CO Alarms

Two distinct alarm requirements at the statute level. Both apply to ADUs.

### § 26F — Smoke Detectors

Applies to dwelling units. Required at:
- Every story (including basements and habitable attics)
- Outside each sleeping area
- In every sleeping room (per IRC R314 as adopted)

**§ 26F also has a sale-stage requirement** — smoke alarm certificate of compliance from the local fire department is required at sale of one- to two-family dwellings. ADU construction triggers permit-stage compliance; subsequent sale of the property may trigger fresh inspection. [source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXX/Chapter148/Section26F | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: MGL Ch 148 § 26F]

### § 26F½ — Carbon Monoxide Alarms

Applies to dwelling units with fuel-burning appliances OR an attached enclosed garage. Required:
- Within 10 ft of every sleeping area
- On every story containing a fuel-burning appliance

CO alarms are NOT required for fully-electric dwelling units with no attached garage (the all-electric Specialized Opt-In Code pathway can eliminate this requirement). [source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXX/Chapter148/Section26F1~2 | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: MGL Ch 148 § 26F½]

### Power Source

- New construction: hardwired with battery backup, interconnected. [source: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXX/Chapter148/Section26F | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: MGL Ch 148 §26F new construction power-source requirement]
- Substantial alteration: typically hardwired plus battery backup.
- Existing without alteration: battery-only sealed 10-year alarms acceptable.

## 527 CMR 1 — Comprehensive Fire Safety Code

### When 527 CMR 1 Triggers In An ADU Permit

The general life-safety code. For most ADUs, the primary intersections are:

- **Means of egress** — overlaps with IRC R311 (egress doors) and R310 (emergency escape openings) as adopted by 780 CMR.
- **Fire department access** — apparatus access roads for parcels with rear-of-lot ADUs may be reviewed by local fire department.
- **Fire flow** — for parcels in outer neighborhoods with limited hydrant coverage, fire flow may be raised by AHJ.

### Sprinkler Requirements

**MA does NOT require IRC R313 residential sprinklers in one- and two-family dwellings**, except by local adoption. Boston has adopted local sprinkler provisions for some construction types; Cambridge has not. Always check the local fire prevention bureau before assuming. [source: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/527-cmr-1-comprehensive-fire-safety-code | retrieved: 2026-05-04 | citation: 527 CMR 1 sprinkler thresholds]

### Common ADU Fire Corrections

- CO alarm omitted on a mixed-fuel ADU (preempted: required under § 26F½)
- Smoke alarm power source called out as battery-only in new construction (incorrect — must be hardwired with battery backup per § 26F new construction provisions)
- Fire-rated assembly not detailed at the dwelling-unit separation between primary and attached ADU (also a 780 CMR / IRC R302.3 issue)
- Apparatus access not shown for rear-of-lot detached ADU on a deep parcel
